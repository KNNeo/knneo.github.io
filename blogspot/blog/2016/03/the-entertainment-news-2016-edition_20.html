ANIME
Assassination Classroom S2: I mean, we're so near the end, and I though this was a two-cour show? What are they going to do after the next half of the season? NOT kill Koro-sensei?? Anyways, back to the episode. The principal (creepy as usual) (Kudos to the voice actor once again) has taken charge of the top class, so he's literally fighting against that octopus, but then his kid Asano-kun is right: Defeat is a way to realise that he's been clinging on too much to victory already. I mean, we know of this since episode one of season one, but what it means here may be more in-depth than it will ever be. He pleads on the End Class to help defeat his classmates to make them wake up somehow, and I guess that's a way of being different than the father, who seriously is a maniac in himself. I mean, Koro-sensei is right in the reasons of education. Who knows what the principal is out for? Oh well. He's keeping the national secret well, he's getting screwed by it too, and he gets a lot of money out of it, so... why not? Haha anyways, can't wait to see a happy ending (I mean, it's the last exam already), and if not... I don't know. Never thought too much into this show in the first place. There are many endings for this though; Not sure where the author will take a stance on this time.
Boku Dake ga Inai Machi: Man, why? And then again, why??
Okay, the story first. So it's years later, and Satoru is alive. For some reason. Easiest assumption is that he somewhat survived enough to be unconscious but not dead, and the mother - being the godliest person in this show - saved his kid and waited for the day to him to become conscious. As if that wasn't enough, the friends and other acquaintances came to meet him. However, all of them came to meet him, and because he cannot remember just yet, the murder suspect of him 15 years ago was here to see him too. Now what? Going by what the Satoru now is going, he's sure to get his ass kicked. I admire his desperation to solve a crime, but then he is at no position to do anything about it. I mean, why doesn't he make use of the people around him? That would've been better. And that would have explained something. That intro story. Have you guys read up on The Spider's Thread? No wonder. The killer was talking about Satoru. His unwillingness to seek help of others he can trust yet only trust himself. See? Mystery solved. Not yet though, 'cause the true story hasn't ended. What will happen to Satoru? We shall see next week. Man, I cannot wait.
Dagashi Kashi Ep9: Maybe it's just Hotaru-san eating too much sweets. I mean, you can have many sweets, but that doesn't make you a kidnapper and murderer lol (Haha wrong show)
Anyways, this episode. Probably dumped in as a filler episode, Hotaru-chan is ill. Down with an ulcer, she takes sweets as her remedy?? Oh well, she still likes sweets anyway, so can we just get that cough syrup for her now? Not about that, the story progression is sure weird as hell, as it starts somewhere and ends up somewhere else. Like, is not eating sweets and taking medicine too normal a things to do for you, Hotaru-chan? Maybe, I guess. She is weird, actually. Someone get her some medicine, please. I like a healthier her, really. Although thinking about that weird girl making those weird pain noises really makes me... aroused? Nah, I guess not. I'm into 3D, not 2D. Sorry, Hotaru-san. *laughs*
(Really, for once I thought Hotaru has a phobia of Kokonotsu lol)
