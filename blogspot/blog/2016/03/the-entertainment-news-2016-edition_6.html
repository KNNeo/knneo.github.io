SHOWS
MythBusters: This is it, huh. Last episode. (Well not really; I'll explain later) 14 years, and probably I've not seen everything that these people gone through. And we're at the end. But of course all things must come to an end, and we don't have to question the reason(s) for it. Still, you must see how many times I tear up already. Let's talk about the Grand Finale episode first (if that is not the first thing you watched first then... oops), shall we? Adam, the great decision maker of MythBusters, decided to send off this show in the best way possible. First, with the biggest use of slow motion cameras. Well, not much when you can only see a fireball and not things flying apart (actually would've been better with Jamie's recommended C4 haha) but that's okay. They decided to send Buster, that sacrifice we never remember to thanks for on this show, the best possible way. And yes, it's to strap moneys worth of rockets on it to send it beyond the speed of sound. It literally disintegrated after hitting a wall. (Wow what a way to make him die lol) Sad, but then again for the last time I will say to it, "Sorry, Buster." Last but not least, we have the much-requested cement truck blowup (and as I said just now if this one is max on ANFO then the first one could've used high explosives instead), and I know I'm satisfied. I'm not the one to ask for replays because the original one can never beat this one in anyway, (as they always say "failure is not an option") but still, it will satisfy all.

The reunion episode (I don't know if it is) aired shortly after that, and it's great to see the five of them together. Talking about their worst times and best times, it's good to know people are genuinely excited for experiments as the fans do despite being on camera for entertainment and science, and that the worst thing in this world is to deal with law enforcement. Which also explains why the most amount of explosives used on this show was 5001 pounds of ANFO. Probably also the most they can allow lol. Anyways, this show has done wonders, has saved lives for the people who watched it, and has inspired many to pursue the scientific method. If it's not Archimedes then MythBusters, who else?
