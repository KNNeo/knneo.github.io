ANIME
Assassination Classroom S2: We meet with another episode of a life lesson, which is using power at the wrong time and... ending up having to save the poor. Okay, not that I don't know of the lessons here unlike the old Sakura-chan, but then again we know it's a mistake. And this level of repayment may be a little too perfect, as the old kind Principal - which is also a victim - mentioned on his evaluation of the campus two weeks later. Also, Karma kicked many students in the ass for the mid-terms of the second half of the final year (just in case anyone is lost in time for this show), which is merely just a small getting back at the tops because these kids had the excuse not to study. Oh well. Rivalry can always take a second chance, besides we still have a couple more episodes to go. Which also means it's not long. But I have a feeling that Koro-sensei won't destroy Earth, and the world won't have to kill him anymore. But then that is always a touching ending, so I'll leave the production responsible for that if it ever happens.
Boku Dake ga Inai Machi: It's halfway already and yet we have more questions to answer.
That is to not say there aren't any questions answered, but then again they are not much. We now know that the crimes of this serial murderer and kidnapper is still investigated by Satoru mother's colleague, and I'm still glad that he still isn't dead yet? Oh well. That and again, Airi is one lucky girl to have potentially escaped death TWICE. I say twice because it's likely she'll not be involved in this again. I mean, if we get an apprehended Satoru and the supposed killer staging the police arrest at the same time, what do you get? Nothing, perhaps. But then again external factors may have been enough (had it not been Airi who tried to meet with Satoru only to find satisfaction and trust) to arrest the culprit, which I have a feeling we might be looking at the whole time. You know. Only he can go through the Revival phenomenon and every time he does that he has time to get back to fix things. Only that probably the one fixing is the present him, which also makes him victim to another of him, which is possible. Really, seeing all those unrealistic stuff in like, the first episode. More questions to ask, and it's creepy, fitting of a mystery story at least. So I guess it's great? More than ever am I focusing on the music and soundtrack in this show (if you still need credit Kajiura-sensei... there you go haha), and I think this will have a befitting ending. Just like Airi, I trust the author and Aniplex (anime production) to give a proper conclusion. And as far as I know, the murderer is no doubt one of the five as listed. I may be wrong, thinking that all this was just his own doing, although that has happened before in drama.
(Sorry for being so long-winded; a lot goes through my mind for such a show, if it isn't this fast-paced)

This Week on Klassic Note
Quite a lot came out this week. Kuso Iinkai's new album showed up, and it isn't disappointing. What is more disappointing is the change in melody, because it's more distinct meaning it feels more... normal. Which is fine, I like normal songs. I'm just stuck in between two new songs I want to give after the topic song (which the music video you should watch on YouTube) in which one is weak at verses and one is weak at the chorus. Goddammit why I have to make these tough decisions every year. Which probably will mean I'll give both by the end of next week. But not this week. More surprisingly, Kuso Iinkai's latest mini-album which was already released like, half a year ago, suddenly surfaced. I haven't gave time to listen to it yet, but from last year's impression from the previews they are not bad. TrySail's 3rd single is also released this week, and I'm holding the B-side for their upcoming album (if any) just because I can. And I haven't even seem the cute music video for the A-side yet... Oh well. I've given Tokyo Karan Koron's new album another song, and I'm not close to confirming anything for pre-order yet.

This is because next week is the release for Tomacchan's new single, in which they are not safe to give per se, but then again I guess I'll probably still get it by next week. We shall see. KANA-BOON's long-awaited album will also release next week, so that's going to get a whole lot of rock music coming my way. üòÜ To add to future releases, Yoru no Honki Dance will release a new album under a major label??
