ANIME
ACCA 13th District: Now the truth is out, I don't know what to say. Even the ending theme featuring (supposedly) Princess Schnee dancing is sad.
In the latest episode (8), turns out that it has always been a plan all along. It's no wonder many around are doubting Jean's identity, well also because why Grossular will be vital in the next part of this story. Later about him, let's talk about a history lesson. (Haha I like how this is only a small part of the King's life haha) (Also the Privy Chairman's) (I wonder who's older lol) It's about thirty years ago that the Princess decided to leave royalty for the world. For there is no one else to fight for the throne the youngest princess who bears a heir shall likely be the successor. And I think we can believe why the King hasn't given up his throne just yet. Surprisingly, Nino's father (I'll take it as the real one) has pledged allegiance to royalty, and serves them to be an observer to the Princess' future lifestyle. Whether it has been marrying a poor man, having two kids (yep you're right they are Jean and Lotta-chan respectively) and then dying in a train accident with Nino's father... it was supposedly all according to plan. I kind of hate if it's like that, but seeing how reaction-less Jean is after hearing the story, I guess I shouldn't be too surprised. Then again, the intro did emphasise well on living the existence, well Nino (who we don't know his previous name) chose to be with his father on his reporting missions to the King, and the Princess did choose to be free. I cannot express enough that you should understand this episode as a decision making point, than think of it as a conspiracy against yourself for not knowing anything. Also, what happens ahead is also pretty unpredictable, considering now many know of this story. Then again Nino is right - Jean is not going to be taking the throne. Or could he? Hey, the King's decision is final, I would say.

Oh well, guess not everything anyone can see, but hey, Shimada is also tired. Of a lot of things. As shown in his dreams, as shown of him hiding from people talking behind his back, as shown of people around him being real a-holes and with "talking about experience" blabbering out of their mouth than along with their brains, it would seem it takes a lot just to be a master in that field. But guess what? No one went as far as Shimada did, so suck it all a-holes. If he didn't see the speck of light in the darkness I'm going to blame on everybody else who didn't put trust in the challenger than that master who probably should be replaced by an AI by now. This Week on Klassic Note
This week is a week of releases. Just as compelling as last week for some, this week is also quite disappointing for a few. Sayuri's new single turned out to be more acceptable than I thought, Yuuki Aira's new single which is also anime ACCA's ending theme is purely just good because of a certain insert song for this week's episode (no, it's really a coincidence), KANA-BOON's new single is not inside my brain yet even though it's the first few that I heard of, and SPECIAL OTHERS collaboration album... let's say I want an instrumental version of the album. Then again, does not remind me AT ALL of the previous album they did with six other groups back in 2011. Really, it doesn't. I'll sort the others out next week, but for now be glad two new singles are going to be only shelf. Stay tuned for reviews in the near future, possibly next weekend.

Update on last week's two albums ended up with me giving Kana-chan's new album just one song (seriously hard to give I would say, even Okii Reiji doesn't stand a chance), and miwa's new album a couple. Really, her album is starting to feel like a movie soundtrack. Maybe it's all the references that has got to do with it. Competition is fierce, miwa-chan, and it's up to you to not write such plain types of songs at this stage of your music career.

Okay, next week. Technically nothing, but if you've been following my Twitter account you'd know of a lot of releases coming in the following weeks and next month, especially. Many are also getting their rare once-in-a-while (or even their first) Anime credits which include THE ORAL CIGARETTES, Ajikan, Yoru no Honki Dance and so on. Literally next season Anime "Eromanga Sensei" who was written by dear manga artist who did Oreimo covers two artists I know of for theme songs: ClariS for opening and TrySail for ending. Next month is going to be great.

Oh, on another important thing I heard of. Appears that in their recent live performances sphere has announced their hiatus on releases and live performances but not other stuff like radio programs (thanks Ayahime for clarifying) "in preparation for 10th anniversary" in 2019. Yeah, they say so, but if you do know of the Anime industry which is literally packed with people who can sing, these girls aren't going to stand a big chance at scoring much considering sales from the past few years haha. Also, the same company also hosts TrySail, their next big thing. Putting that into account, you'd know why they want to focus on the juniors instead. But seriously, I'll miss them. As a group. Individually... hey I know I'm starting to like Ayahi-chan haha
