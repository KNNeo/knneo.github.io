ORESAMA - Gimmme! [Single]
(Well, you just need to know that, just like their other releases, these are very ORESAMA so read on to find out why)

Origin: Finally I get to talk about them in all their glory from indie to major and how I've heard of them all these years. Everyone, meet ORESAMA, an indie duo a long time coming. They started years ago as an indie group, with the guy writing songs and Pon-san our dear female vocalist singing them. As you can hear for yourself, they specialise in dance pop, not exactly a popular genre out there, kind of niche, but they got recognition real fast. Also for an Anime theme song for "Alice to Zouroku", they major debuted in 2017 with their 1st major single, and that probably broke all the records they have so far and many years to come. For previously they were depicted through drawings and animations, major debut allowed of them to grace their appearance in front of the camera, as they have been in one of the best music videos I've seen ever since, no joke. Fast forward beyond their boom until after their 1st album which died down real fast in Klassic Note, I still pop in from time to time to see if they have any great songs to offer, and this one I can't miss. It's for Anime too, but unlike the comedy series itself which is a joke, this is such a wonderful song for an ending, it's almost unfitting for it. Let's talk about why.

(1) Gimmme!
From first chorus:
"Ah call it good night
To be amazed, (at the extent of) the brightness of this world
This bleak happiness, (I) see in (my) dreams"
For being an Anime song, the lyrics easily fit into the theme real well, since it's mostly about dreams and sleep. But it's more like having a good night in the night club but you'd rather spend it in your bed, of sorts. The song itself might be simple in melody, but it's catchy and easy to take in, so you can't say anything bad about it once you get into it. As mentioned earlier, it takes a lot to like this dance pop style, and if you can take it, the whole package of this song is actually real good, to be honest, and might be their best in a while. The music video is only going to make you like this song more and especially, their vocalist Pon-san even more with all the cosplay and fainting.
Rating: 4 stars (Quirky Pop Average)

(2) Yakou no Ame [Rain of Night Stroll]
"Dear rain don't go, don't leave me alone and go
The scars that don't disappear and stir up (from time to time), again alone, (for the) rest of the night"
This, is definitely changing my impression of ORESAMA, for the better. My typical impression of them is like of a dance club: happy, joyful, having a great time, and they are entertaining us through music. But this, is one of those sides of them you hardly will see, and that I think above all else, is why I liked this song. Through it's slow beats and meaningful lyrics, this truly represents the feels of being alone and sad in the rain, walking through the night. This loneliness, I'm sure you have felt it if you tried staying up late. Hallucinations begin to form, solitude becomes a necessity. I think just walking down the feels of this tune, I can totally feel that running through me. Don't try to put this on the next lo-fi pop radio YouTube video, I tell you; They deserve more than that. I would argue though, that the quieter moments of this song might be too quiet, as heard from the beginning and bridge parts, which may or may not be a plus for this song.
Rating: 4 stars (Lonely Average)

Conclusion: I doubt we will ever see another perfect release like this one from ORESAMA, but I trust they will deliver something better than this in the future. Meanwhile, if no one has heard of them even outside of Anime theme songs, be sure to support them and give them a listen if you like their style. This style isn't everywhere in the Japanese music industry, and I think they make of perfect fit for Anime. The major debut only made them shine further into the light.

Ueda Reina - Literature [Single]
Artist Edition
 Anime Edition
(I didn't have a choice the Artist's Edition had one more track which is as good as none so I had to take the Anime Edition. I think we got over that aesthetic back in 2014 already so don't mind Elaina if you don't know her)

Origin: Okay, about why we are even here in the first place. To be frank? I don't even know much of Ueda Reina. You may think this is a joke, but if not for the Anime, I wouldn't even give this a listen. But since we're here means something happened between her and her other voice actress friends that have made her voice sound so well, let her show off her singing more than anyone else, and brought her appeal to me. I don't even know if this is her usual style in her years of solo music, but I can tell you, this is truly an impression on many levels. If you think she sounds like Hanazawa Kana or Hondo Kaede, you're not alone. Let's see if we can find out more about what her music career is with this release.

(1) Literature
"Where are you going?
...Slightly far away
Leaving behind things (memories) for my dreams
Choosing what (I) like
Because (freedom of) choice is what will make of me"
It's an Anime theme song for a series I've been watching "Majou no Tabitabi", and if I say the lyrics need a compatibility test because the lyricist literally nailed this too well, I may still be underestimating my words. Every corner of this song is a description of Elaina the main girl and the world around her as she travels and doesn't say much about the things around her, just enjoying travel, helping out others, and writing her fascinations in her diary. The lyrics covered everything, I'm not even exaggerating. Kudos though, to the music producer, a seasoned Anime producer who also packaged this song that will make of impact, though swift and smooth, though soft and sweet. A true example of the power of music in face of Anime, and I must say this is a very good attempt. I may have been harsh on the bridge for being too high on pitch or off notes for some in the chorus when she sings, but maybe the melody is not fitting for the singer herself. But if this is meant as a healing song, then it has done it, with impact even, to the level of the music video too.
Rating: 4 stars (Anime Standard Average)

(2) Hana no Ame [Rain of Flowers]
"Summer without you (I) feel slightly lonely
Passing through (days in) the long spring (I) will continue to sing
(I) will not lie Tu lala lila"
Yes, this was the one which blew my mind. Let's just say I've not heard of a soothing melody all year, and this one threw me off my rails, and then some. The lyrics ensure that I never forget the feels of seasons changing, and also, a cute and lonely girl's heart shouting out for company. Coupled with Ueda Reina's soothing and soft voice, this might be too suitable of a song for her. I would like to think the composer is also of such calibre writing in such a style, picturing the music going along with Reina-chan feeling for her own written lyrics... Now you know why my fear for giving this single went away as soon as they played nice to me. All the way too, except maybe at the end not so much, as it could have had a better ending. Still, this makes of a lovely song I would find once in a while in mainstream, and I will never expect to find again unless Toyosaki Aki came up with another single. Hey, she filled in that gap, what can I say. The music video is a special project where they asked for fans to upload photos of two-flower arrangements and it was patched into a music video sequence.
Rating: 4 stars (Lovingly Above Average)

Conclusion: All in all, I came in from doubtful and just wanting to give the Anime I watch its opening theme song, to now I think she is idolism material. What can I say? I'm already too into it by now, just like how just two songs can change my mind about voice actress Ueda Reina, and I think Reina-chan, I'm pleased to have met your acquaintance. Even if you have not heard of her, this single brings about a soothing feel that one will not easily forget, so be sure to immerse yourselves in a world of Anime... and flowers and Ueda Reina herself, why not.


-Kaineng, on the Klassic Note
