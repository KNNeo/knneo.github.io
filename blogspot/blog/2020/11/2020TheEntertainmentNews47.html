Êº´Áîª Boku ga Boku de Aru Tame ni.
I feel like I should come back to this, and I'm glad I did.

Hello, everyone, to the... definitely once in a while long manga review, but here, is the last of this series. Yes, after 5 years of serialisation, it has ended. With the final volumes out this month (yes Volumes 8 and 9 were released on Friday actually), I have decided to show my support for the artist, but unlike Kawai Makoto-sensei, for Hazuki Matcha-sensei here also the author for "Isshuukan Friends." (yes "One Week Friends") I have decided to skip the shipping queue (also it's not cheap) and read it up on Amazon Kindle, so not sponsored even though I got a good deal on all NINE volumes here, but let's talk everything about it.
Trust me, it's not that you think, in both cases haha
(Is that girl sucking his d-)
Story goes that Shun-kun has came back to Tokyo after years of leaving his bunch of friends from middle school those years ago (it has a pattern, so ~4 years I believe, we'll talk about that later), finally coming back to meet up with them... in high school, of course, and with many things upon them, they talk about the situation now. But instead of finding them all together, they are all over the place, probably left from fragments of their childhood. And Shun-kun - not being able to face his own emotions and of course, his emotions for childhood friend Sana-chan - meets with her new friend in school Ayumu... who looks exactly like him. Exactly might be an understatement, because the moment they put that misunderstanding into action ie. Shun-kun taking off his glasses, people around them start to get confused immediately. (Okay besides the one guy who know of the pin on Ayumu) And thus, Ayumu suggested such a play around, but Shun decided then, to use this as a weapon to understand the feelings of others, as much of a wimp of himself and his image of not wanting to face his emotions could be.
That is Sana-chan on the left (kawaii~), and Shun-kun on the right.
(Trust me, this is not Ayumu lol this was after being found out by Yuu-kun)
Seems that it's pretty straightforward on what is upon us, I hope. We got main guy who likes main girl, some other guy he can pass off as to effectively eavesdrop on the others around him to find out what happened these years along, and rounding back to what lives they had before Shun-kun left with his family. And spoiler alert from now onwards because you can wait for translations if you don't mind lol, but yeah, it happened again, the leaving. NOW we can talk about the strengths and weaknesses about this series, and Matcha-sensei's approach in general. (1) Don't expect wholesome stories. Like the author's previous work, the focus here is only on emphasising on the waves of emotions and amplifying them only for enough for something to happen. The strongest of drama is probably nothing compared to anything you watch on cable TV, trust me. That, in my opinion, I see as a bias because I actually like such a style, because in Japan such a genre is rather niche but otherwise also treasured("kyunkyun"), because you don't have to be too direct about love yet still be able to do everything about it. If you aren't into such troubled and missed emotions then this is hard to actually convince anyone to understand, just like how the emotions of all the characters are hard to understand until you hear their stories. (2) The turning points for this series. It's never ground breaking, and it's most of the time, rather expected to some extent. Most of the time if not all the time, it's meeting up to talk about their stories, and never using those clich√© methods of encounters all the time like many stories would use, to come to conflict or conclusion. But hey, this really depends on how you approach it though. And yeah, in the end, these stuff are kind of necessary anyways, so I won't say too much. (3) The ending. As I said, it happened again, Shun-kun has to leave with the family again because the father has to, and I'm not going to investigate that part because I know of Anime that will literally confront the father and do the runaway thing, but hey, this story is not that. Focus here is then, instead of just walking about of the window and being honest about each other like the reveal of Shun-kun's switches between Ayumu to Sana-chan and Kouhei, he then chose instinctively, out of his own wimpy self, to keep mum about everything to everyone yet again. Most who read this would think of how much of a wimp he is then to do so even though we all know that hurting others of such news once again, things such as farewell, is not a good idea, and as much as it's also not of good idea to keep mum about it. I would say the latter is worse, but you need to then remember the characters of this series aren't a bunch of strong-willed people. If you think of it that way, and that's why the author chose such an ending instead, a more roundabout approach. But despite the teacher ratting him out accidentally mistaking Ayumu as Shun-kun again (lol sensei), and why wouldn't you also block Ayumu on your phone after blocking everyone else, Sana-chan at least, still managed to say her goodbyes to... his bestie. Yeah. Yes, the problem with this series is that you cannot be sure who are together, because they are never, even after 4 years down the road where everyone is in college and stuff and Shun-kun still asks for Sana-chan to wait a little longer for his action. Even though...!! Even though Sana-chan has dropped such a big hint on him. Ugh, I can't even. üòÜ I'll take that as a yes, guys; I can live without the actual confession haha
Hey, congrats bro, congrats. She said it before you you loser-
All in all, I like this series. As I mentioned in the past few reviews over the years as I see it serialised in Japanese online to be read for free as the latest chapters come out and I missed most of it lol, the last time I read this was like, around the end of Volume 6, as I surprisingly, picked up from where Shun-kun decided to confess about his switching with his lookalike. So yeah, that's a good point to continue and then find out what the end would be like. Then again, I am disappointed that, despite opening the time capsule and then Sana-chan finding out that through opening Shun-kun's box with his key entrusted to her as he tries to sneak out forever haha (did I explain that for you?), it's rather obvious that Shun still likes Sana-chan, and not just as a friend, and similarly, the girl should have felt that too, despite being switched around with maybe Ayumu on some occasions. See? It's not that hard being yourself. *shakes head* That's the only grime I have with this series, I guess, to then find out that that's literally what the title is about, and yes, they will not easily change because in all the stories of the characters mentioned throughout, it's always about them refusing to change. But changing is literally, being weird for a while, and then that is the new norm. And that's how Kouhei moved on after quitting the running club because the club members probably were involved in his incident where he injured his leg, and how Sana-chan got over being lied to by Shun-kun over the switching. That's how it is. In the latter she probably cried a lot (lol), but she also moved on. That's how life should be. I wouldn't say this is how you get laid with someone you like lol Chihiro, but hey, it's some feelings one wouldn't easily forget. I would argue that the author tried the very best to like, how should I say it, display these wavering emotions on the characters to make you feel sentimental, sometimes shocked, but mostly uneasy, about the feelings you have for others and what they are. And this, is what it feels like to be yourself. And it serves to be a good reminder to me too, after all these years of being alone.
"I keep behaving weird, I keep doing things that aren't of who I am, but even then, I have not disliked that feeling"
TL:DR; Don't lie to others, be what you are always, and accept change through the best of your understanding. Even if it makes you feel worse for yourself and others around you, embrace those emotions that make you you, for others will do so too.
