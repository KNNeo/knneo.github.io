Suzuki Masayuki - GIRI GIRI feat. Suu [Single]
Limited Edition Regular Edition
(I mean, it is for Anime, and Suzuki-san here isn't exactly attractive for kids like me so... nah he's cool)

Origin: Let's read the inlet of this CD for a better understanding of his 40th year in singing - Anison world's "Forever Newbie" arrives for the third time! So if you don't know of dear Suzuki Masayuki right here no one will blame you for it; Your dad if he's Japanese probably knew him better than you do. Besides singing funky songs as his usual style over the years, about 5 years ago he has been brought in my Anime producers to sing the opening theme for Anime "Kaguya-sama" and launched him into unprecedented territory where now he has the power to attract you and your mom at the same time! Ironically perfect for the budget, the third time around brings his release into a single unlike previous releases for the Anime, which is why I get to talk about it.

(1) GIRI GIRI feat. Suu [Just Barely]
From the first chorus:
"Just barely let (us) love each other
Burn those lies away, let it be dreams
(I'm) not joking, let's dance more
Damn (I) can't take it anymore, let's confess (our) love, love, love"
This song and every opening theme song Suzuki-san has sung for this series has been on point, from its unexpected funky style to the lyrics mocking about the romance between the main characters because spoiler alert we are three seasons in and it's "just barely" as hinted by the song title, we are welcome with another great melody that will sing to you as you remember this series. It's not going to surprise anyone the third time coming, but with another guest vocalist to add to the mix, this time welcoming in SILENT SIREN's vocalist Suu whom you might know, is here to deliver the lighter portion of the vocal scale. The lyrics of course hint about being ace student, a bit of teasing here and there, and how much love wants to exist in between. And this will never get old, in my opinion.
Rating: Just Barely Average

Conclusion: This will be a classic to remember, you heard it first. Whether you should bring your parents or even grandparents to like this too is up to you, but I'm sure with the backing of Anime, we all will remember how close we have come to giving one of the oldest music artists you might have listened to at one point. I'm not saying this is bad, but it will be an experience to live with.

Suzuki Airi - Heart wo Oteage [Single]
Limited Edition Regular Edition
(Yes, artist cover, don't ask more questions)

Origin: For having to be guest vocal in a former Suzuki Masayuki song for the Anime's season 2, this former idol group member has come a long way for her solo music career, and this single marks her major debut! That of course sounds easy considering this is also her first Anime credit, which makes only her exposure to a larger audience greater than ever, among the other things about this attractive woman with a great singing voice, I can believe this is one of many releases that will put her under the spotlight.

(1) Heart wo Oteage [Giving Up (My) Heart]
From the chorus:
"Can't put into words about the clumsy you
(I) shall embrace everything, jumping over the borders, (I) can't stop anymore
Giving up (My) heart"
We are quickly presented with a simple yet catchy melody of a romance song, you cannot get better than this quality of music. Saying that though, it does still suffer from the usual long bridge part issues which I will not elaborate, but the verses and chorus will make sure you remember this song at length, one will never complain how good this just sounds. With the slight slurs and her powerful voice, Suzuki Airi here is quick to deliver great singing to this nice song. The lyrics of course touch more on the Anime itself, but also describe looking from the back of ones you love, being speechless with plenty of affection, this only says a lot more about what this love song is, devoting one's heart to another.
Rating: Lovingly Average

Conclusion: I feel like this is a great exposure to this idol singer, so don't get me wrong when I say I'm more attracted to her singing than to her in her image. For music is presented at its best without image, this just makes it more than appealing to give after the wonderful melody and lyrics which are on point. This package just overall feels like such a treat, fans of the Anime series cannot take a miss on this treasure.


-Aozaki Shouhei, on the Klassic Note
