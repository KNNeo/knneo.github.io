SHE'S - Blue Thermal [Single]
Limited Edition Regular Edition
(I mean, The Regular Edition cover is a bunch of balloons behind a punched hole of a thin wall behind a layer of plastic and bubbles? Artistic, to say the least)

Origin: I have to say, I should thank SHE'S for bringing me closer to feminism than I will ever be with their music. Being soft with their music but bringing impact on everything they create, they just tend to exude a little more of those feelings from the female perspective better than any other male artist(s) could ever within the industry, and I applaud them for that. Their style also brought me to like them more than ever since I knew of them five years ago, with singles to love and album to please. This recent single is no doubt just credited for the animated movie of the same title, and I can say this is the easiest way to exhibit how their style doesn't fade when being associated with credit, as they are still being able to stand on edge with their music.

(1) Blue Thermal
From the first chorus:
"The pains that pierce the heart
Draw the curves that the wind carries
Admiring, riding and overcoming, flying up high
(I) will take you along"
As you might have figured out from the lyrics, the song sings about flying in the sky, ambitions to soar in the blue world above us, with power and with company. For having inherited the song title from its credit, it's hard to think that it should be more relatable to the show itself, but surprisingly they are still able to create such a good mood out of it, and I admire them for that. For the melody, it does borrow the vibes of epic tunes they have written over the years, brought in with impact and with much love. Overall a nice package for a song you cannot screw up on, and hearing it more just makes me feel guilty for preferring their classics over this. I also managed to watch the music video premier on YouTube on time, and it paints a blue picture of teen hope and courage as a statement to follow for the movie.
Rating: Beautifully Above Average

(2) Beautiful Bird
From the first verse:
"I know,I know,I know
Seeing (my) footsteps
(The) Things enough to make (me) afraid and unable to proceed
So I walk,I walk,I walk
I walk straight without a stop
When (I) see you that was what (I) decided"
This song... this feels like a song of praise. Praise them birds, or praise them braving the skies... this song feel like a song of encouragement, once again, with company. The emotions are strong on this one, which really made me think twice the first time I heard of this. Its simple melody and impactful lyrics did leave an impression on me, and with much thought I accepted it and gave them praise for making such simple yet nice music once again, as they have been doing all these years. The English in the lyrics just make them stand out a little more than you might imagine.
Rating: Simply Average

Conclusion: This is a perfect single to show how great SHE'S actually is, and I encourage you to give them a listen. I often say that if you can like at least two styles of a band you will love them, and this single has both of them. Of impact, and of emotion, these guys handle them all gracefully and with style. What more can I say? I am sure coming out from watching the animation this will bring an even greater impact, but I don't even need that to know these guys gave it their all.

- Aozaki Shouhei, on the Klassic Note
