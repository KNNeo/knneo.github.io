I'd like to call the final episode (12) #TheTruth but yeah, there's no truth. Let me get the gist of the story out of the place so I can do that appreciation post I do every season lol. If I were Hana-chan, and after this much, and I dreamt of that? I'll sit them down and tell them. Mainly because I'll also not have those weird dreams anymore, and also, to relieve a place in my heart. Very important for self-development, by the way. (Yes, I know this is giving place to come back with a sequel if necessary and that's how ALL Anime adaptations end, but still) Okay, everything great about this show. If the postscripts and thumbnails below are not convincing enough then I'll tell you more haha. Firstly, opening theme song. Unlike the regular ending theme song and the special one we hear this episode, this has a special place in defining what the show is, and thanks to production company Aniplex we get to be blessed with such wonderful music. It's as Anime as it can get, people. Secondly, the mood. The pace may be slow, but the mood is great. Never stops to give us an insight to everyone in the group, little stories which can matter so much you know if Eiko and the teacher walk down the alley you know something's gotta happen, and that creep behind the girls who are spying on the two lol. See? This is called "plot depth", everyone. Then, there's the reality check. Thanks to the accounts of Hana-chan and Hiroe-san mainly, the story focuses on delivering the truth to some extent, showing the struggles of being a student and especially, falling behind. Hey, it's no live-changing movie but this is good to make me tear up, so I give that a thumbs up. Lastly, everything else I missed. Not talking about girls' love, not really talking about nudity, and most definitely not talking about how much fun they have together and all the jokes that come together. If the Anime wasn't planned this way I may already have left three episodes in. If you haven't watched it this is good time to. And if you had, I hope you had much laughs (and tears) as I did. Hana-chan is the one who started it all. You can thank her for... all the stuff I'd rather you experience. That, or I can't remember all of them. Hey, that's feature-packed content right there haha

Well I guess you heard of UNISON SQUARE GARDEN and RUANN last week, so I'll not bore you any further. This week, I had some time to review just two albums, and I think I got enough to talk about them already. I finally got to hear Ryokushaka's 1st album, and hmm... not as great as I thought. It's hard to give them songs mostly because of the keyboard tunes, I guess. Then again, the melodies in the songs are very inconsistent and are those long ones which I don't like because I think it doesn't fit in the song (exceptions do apply, but no exceptions here), and in general I would like ot hear something stronger. In emotion, mainly. The emotional songs hardly hit me, and the lively ones... I don't know. I can feel the familiarity to J-Pop, but it did not hit me tbh. Same thing with FLOWER FLOWER's new album after three years too, and that's saying something. Then again, I've determined this to be the last release I'm reviewing before leaving them on the edge because I've seriously not gotten used to their style considering the topic songs they have chosen. The give-birth song probably hits more mainly because of the melody, but yeah yui-san it's not much. I may just give a couple of songs though unlike Ryokushaka, and that's probably as much as I'll give them.
