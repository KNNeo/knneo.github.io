ANIME FOR FALL 2014 (and a one-liner!)
Denkigai no Honya-san: This one… apparently is of value to watch. As from Random Curiosity (not that I have to quote them, but it’s for your reference. And I’m abbreviating the name from here) if you’re a fan of “WORKING!!” with some “Seitokai Yakuindomo standard” of jokes (it’s a bookstore in Japan anyway), then this show will be great, especially for me, so I will just watch it.
Theme songs I have mentioned just one, which is Ayachi who (is also in the cast) is doing the opening (if you need to really listen the preview’s online), and the Anime cast doing the ending, all the females apparently. Not concerned of the theme songs this time round; I’m going for storyline, and a lot of comedy.
Shigatsu wa Kimi no Uso: Well this is another one that I would definitely watch, even for its first episode. Adapting from an award-winning manga, this is a love story about a 14-year-old male (yeah he’s high school already I guess) who apparently cannot feel music until he met this girl. Sounds like a premise for good songs and romance all over the place, so I’m not giving this a miss.
Theme songs wise… Goose house (you guys may have heard of them; I talked about them and their previous song was for Anime “Gin no Saji”) is doing up the opening song along with, I heard, a cover compilation with the show itself, so this is already opening up the possibilities we get form the acoustic pop group. I do not know who is doing the ending song, but I’m interested to give chance. The last time I did I digged up their latest album and already pressed the button for the single released. (more to come later). This is more than storyline, people. It’s for the impression of romance. And I bet it’s going to be a potentially good one. Even people at RC says it has chance for top show this season. Well, excluding the sequels, of course.
Ushinawareta Mirai wo Motomete: The reason? Well for first it’s the theme song; Satomi-chan’s second single is here. And from the PV it seems it’s going to be rather different in style. The show? I’ll see about it. It looks like some harem, but on the other hand the storyline portrays doing the last school festival in old school grounds, so I suppose I can try to close one eye on the phyical interactions (if potentially any) in this show. I’ll just have to see. Yes, I’m just hanging around here because of the theme song; Everything else will have to see.Sora no Method: Apparently all the shows I choose to watch from since I don’t know when (Titans, maybe?) till now have not been in any action genre. That doesn’t matter, I just like to see weird shows like this that modern-day drama cannot provide. This talks about a childhood wish made by a bunch of girls, and apparenly came true in the form of a disc in the sky? Yeah I know it’s weird, and it’s an Anime original, but I’ve seen Glasslip, so what harm would it do if I watch this? I will take extreme caution in accepting this show though; I only came here because of the cast (Aki-chan, Mikakoshi) and I heard the ending song is done by fhana. Yeah, pretty much it. Nevertheless I still have high hopes, especially if I do intend to watch it. Always when on the surface it’s awesome means to cover something in the inside. Watch Glasslip for details, haha.
