(I hardly watch drama anyway but I did watch two of them, namely "Omae wa Mada Gunma wo Shiranai" in 2017 and "Shinya no Damekoi Zukan" in 2018) I knew her through gravure, and if you didn't notice below because she has a respectable rack, along with her initial boom back around 4 years ago with those wonderful - to the extent of enticing - shots, and I've been following her ever since. THE INTERVIEW
It's not a long one, but I think I should put this in. So, to talk about the main points which focused mostly about Fumika-chan on travelling instead of being a self-introduction interview:
  • She thought the (baby) koala would be hard (lol) but it's real soft and warm, and cute.
  • "There are koalas in Japan zoos but I've only seen my friends hug it before and say it's good" Haha nice joke Fumika-chan
  • When asked to rank the animals in Australia ie. koala, wallaby and kangaroo, she likes koala and kangaroos because they are cute, but it's her inherent fear of seeing kangaroos jump towards her that gave koalas the 1st place.
  • On sitting on a boat from Cannes to Melbourne, she said she couldn't stand the shaking even to the extent the kid beside her was puking, and she felt bad for him/her.
  • The photobook was focused around "amazing scenes" so it was bad when the crew met with no sunny weather the only day they had to do the photo shoot by the beach. Fumika-chan says it's inevitable and the temperature isn't exactly warm either at the time of year. (It was August when they did the shoot)
  • Cannes may be cold, but arguably Melbourne is colder. They knew about how people could get away with half sleeved outfits in the day but it was colder than expected, and they had to take gravure level shots too? It was Fumika-chan who suggest it too, as she jokes laughingly at herself. (Bear in mind this was mostly planned by her)
  • On being asked why go to Melbourne, Fumika-chan heard from a friend that the atmosphere is great and it's only a 3 hour plane ride (which she is bad at, ooo) from Cannes too.
  • She thinks the streets of Melbourne are "cute", and there are plenty of unique shops around. Melbourne also has longer operating hours since in Cannes most shops or restaurants will close by around 9pm, which makes of a very laid back experience, and she would like to visit again.
  • Coincidentally, this photobook was released 5 years after her first gravure shoot in November 2014. On being asked where she would like to go in another 5 years, Fumika-chan said ask her then.
