I would argue that one of the reasons for me to play that game till date is her, but also because I've since then heard her in various roles in many Anime she stars in, from "Subete ga F ni Naru" (if you recognise which role she voiced without looking at the credits you are a true fan) to "Eromanga-sensei", where she acts a "fashion b*tch" lmao. It was only until recently that she announced an original photobook featuring nothing but her, in a suggestively "more mature" theme as she turned 21 last year (how wrong did this sound lol) and before this she was hardly ever seen in such an image. THE INTERVIEW
Well this book also had one, and referring back to Marinka this was also her first book (thanks Nan-chan for not joining in haha) we HAVE to talk about her life lol. Sure, I'll do it, Ibuki-chan. Here's again, a summary of the conversation:
(If it's lesser than Marinka then it means that I've lost some interest lol)
[*All entertainment references mentioned below are searchable if you are interested]
  • In this interview she was still 20? So this photobook was prepared more than half a year ago? Hmm...
  • She is Kido Ibuki (lol), she likes to eat sea pineapple (not into seafood so...) since she was a kid (uh oh, how young are we talking here?) but she likes to put a lot of salt which made her face all swollen wtf
  • Her hobby is to watch local blockbuster drama, and stated "N no Tame ni" as an example. Typically she likes to watch drama with cringy storylines (yeah same but I only watch animation)
  • She read a lot of shoujo manga as a kid, and liked "Vampire Knight" the most. She also liked "Koi wa Ameagari no You ni" (I know of that but didn't watch) (so she likes middle aged guys? haha) She has always read such manga as a kid so she has grown to like such shoujo romance manga
  • She likes dress-up type video games (uh oh) and stated "Animal Crossing" as a recent example. She hardly plays games even though many around her are into that work (voicing for video games) so she feels out of place when talking about this. She stated games like "Wagamama Fashion GIRLS MODE" (known as "Style Savvy" outside Japan, for the DS)Â  and plays that all day. She is determined to clear all daily objectives as she was always pressed (pun intended) to hit the "Next" button in the game (likely for the story as some games need to play a certain amount before more plot reveals)
  • She has been taking up cooking in order to eat healthy recently (aw...) mainly because when she came into Tokyo she has been taken care of by the agency and now she is just being independent and doing it to better take care of her health. (What have they been feeding her...) Her best is making vegetable soup (she can feed me now haha)
  • As a kid, she has been playing a lot of playing house (yes it's a thing), and particular is into playing those role-playing toys. She is outgoing with others as a kid and was quite an attention seeker, and the adults around her was all praises of her cuteness. Her mother often dresses her in pink and takes care of her hair too (as seen from the childhood photos above)
  • She started learning piano when she was 4. She got influenced when it was time to keep the toys in kindergarden and the piano was nearby where students started learning there, so the teacher told her to ignore keeping the toys one day and just join in. (Good teacher, this one haha) She gave up after 3 months. She said she is still confident enough to play some kid songs (not mentioning names here I got tired of research haha) with two hands
  • She was good at drawing in school, and even drew her own version of "Mahou Shoujo Kirari-chan" after reading the first chapter of the manga (wow that's impressive)
  • She knew of the seiyuu career when she was in elementary school years 4-5 (that's around 10). Back then she was watching Anime "Shugo Chara!" and liked one of the voices of the characters a lot, and after much research knew about the voices behind Anime characters. After finding out it was voiced by (pure coincidence here, see image below after interview) Nana Mizuki she got even more interested in the job. At first it was just innocent watching of Anime as a fan, but soon her curiosity became the motivation of wanting to take on such a career. She would rush home from school to read the latest manga, and even did her own voicing to some of the characters in them.
  • She knew of the audition of her current agency (Horipro) from her mother's friend, and registered for the audition herself. (Wow) She received a short period of dance, acting and singing lessons and after all of this the agency invited her to join and she immediately agreed. However she was in middle school back then and her parents advised her to complete high school first, but her passion won over their hearts and she proceeded with support from the family. (Aw...)
  • She was (undoubtedly) uneasy to have to leave hometown (Aomori, FYI) to work in Tokyo. But most of her was like in "From here on shining days will be waiting upon me!" type of feelings. She struggled to cope with transport as Aomori had little rail transport (only trams) and was surprised with city life in Tokyo with sophisticated subway systems and crowds of people in city centers. But she stayed strong as her then manager guided her along.
  • She struggled to do well in auditions for Anime (in a nutshell seiyuu audition for their roles they don't just hand it to you) but continued to believe that she will get it someday. For the people around her age who got accepted into those roles were also there supporting and encouraging her, giving her motivation to strive on.
  • She started as a seiyuu officially in October of 2012. It was also her first female lead role, the character was coincidentally around her age (bear in mind her age at this point) (she was 15), and coincidentally it had an elder brother like herself, so she was able to get right into it. (It's Anime "OniAi" as mentioned by the interviewer, and her role has a brother complex. I want to be her brother irl now haha)
  • She didn't believe she got that role (for "OniAi") as she flubbed many times and her role demanded many monologues in which she can't speak fast fluently (also called "hayakuchi") so she ended up slowing down her words, but the production told her that since she was putting in the effort to act out the role they gave in to that weakness instead. (Flexibility in Anime production, ladies and gentlemen) She thought she did badly, but her effort did pay off. It did take her a while to catch up to the standards required in the studio (probably doing more takes) with the other actors watching so she felt bad about it. She couldn't figure out what she needed to improved on, and took a lot of time to grasp her role. It was Shimoda Asami who helped her out and taught her many things along the way, so she was thankful for her. (Shimoda acted in it as the main guy's childhood friend btw) Through this Anime, she learned of many things about the job that she knew was necessary in her career from then on.
  • She liked singing since she was a kid, and mostly sang Anime songs. (Yes Nana Mizuki, she liked your "ETERNAL BLAZE") (I feel so young now lol) She had no experience in dancing, so she spent more of the time to master that.
  • Her best impression of acting a character was (don't guess) from "Idolmaster Million Live!", and thanks to her role she got plenty of experience performing music live in front of an audience. (In Idolmaster almost all actresses have to participate in live events and sing and dance and so on, just like an idol would) She also learnt in particular, to smile in front of the audience when singing.
  • She wants to try on boyish roles as she has done many lively girl roles over the years. (Nope, imouto-chan Ibuki-chan you're stuck here now haha)
  • She is keen on acting in musicals as she has done a couple of times, but feels that the daily performances can take a toll on her health (she really might have an issue here), but she did enjoy acting with others a lot on stage so she wants to do more in the future. She also got a chance to act briefly in a real life movie (this I know from her Twitter, called "Kimi to, Tsurezure"), felt it was rushed for schedule so the experience was short, but wished she got more chances for those.
  • To a letter of her in the future: "Are you doing well? Are you married? (gulp) Are you still working hard as a seiyuu? (Continue to) Grow up and be a perfect and matured woman!"
  • To fans: "This was the first time you saw a lot of different sides to Kido Ibuki. How was it? (It was... good!) I'll work hard to continue to act various roles in the future, so continue to support me!"
