In the final episode (#DomeKano12) of this really, really sad to end way of a series, we end at a part that... is conclusive of what happened mainly in this arc, which is Hina-chan and her relationship with men. Well, mostly Natsuo but I have to include the others into the equation so if you'll excuse me. Because of those strong feelings and Hina's character and the student board choosing the older person to approach first, things changed real fast after it was discovered that Hina-chan was having a relationship with Natsuo, ie. student-teacher relationship. You know, those that aren't acceptable by society. Again, to hell with society but still, it was an issue. So since the choice landed on Hina's hands, she took it to leave the school and Natsuo in order to not ruin his future. And in that, even Natsuo, I think everyone handled her passing real well. It's those "I know it's for my own good but" and then Natsuo just can't stop crying and screaming and not forget about the times they were together... understandable for a typical breakup plot without looking at the face for some reason (well I can't imagine if there is physical contact, so...) so I'm not going to complain about the plot line. At the very least the author and production handled it well. Moving on, Natsuo had to pour out all his overwhelming feelings of sorrow and pain into a novel, and in doing so got him what he didn't expect: a winning prize in a literary award. Hey, congrats, bro. Now is to remember that feeling and write stories with it. What is great about life is that on remembering things you have done, and if it was remembered well and vividly, can be motivation for you to conitnue living. That, I couldn't have experienced it better myself, and you probably did too even without love. Hina-chan chose one of the right paths to not tarnish any of their reputations in public, and in embracing that sacrificed what would be important in keeping Natsuo's dream safe, so he should thanks her for that. Lastly, it's Rui. Probably the most heartbroken because he did it once with Natsuo and has been till now only suppressed her feelings for the only true man in the story (lol sorry Marie and your half body tattoo), is now free. Free of suppression, for starters. Whether Natsuo will take that in like he does in substitution to another like he always does, is up to him. And of course up to whether we'll get a second season of Anime because it stops there. Yep, that's it, folks. Overall, I enjoyed the series. I know about the obvious sex and physical contact scenes they gotta put in to justify the adaptation, but even so they have done it enough to not make me walk away (well it'll be more than this before I do anyway) or engrossed that I want to j**k off to it. Okay, not that really, but I enjoyed the drama the most. They may not have enough character development outside of the main character scope (for example the parents questioning their marriage again, Miu-chan on Kiriya etc.), but otherwise it was a good show. And to be honest? I can't take any more cheesy sorrows or cry-outs besides this more understanding approach of like venting it out and moving on, making this the most matured ending to a sad romance story I've seen so far in my life in Anime. And for that, Sasuga-sensei, thanks for this. And Good Ending. That may not have been a hit, but this did. And I'm glad to have remembered your pen name. Hey, look Natsuo's more well-known than you now haha jkjk
