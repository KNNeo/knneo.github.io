Dagashi Kashi: More sweets, chests, and awesome shocks this episode, as we embark the slow-paced yet very entertaining show that is talking about the origin and many antics of the sweetest stuff we eat. Honestly, catching up to what I know of in the Manga already, but then that's good, because I won't know what's next. Boku Dake no Inai Machi: The mystery gets further, deep into the abyss. Well, unless you think like me, you probably won't. For one, these shows make me think. If you had wondered why it was 18 years ago if it had to be a "Revival" phenomenon, and why Kayo-chan ('cos she's cute xD) knows of loneliness at 10 years old. Well I guess we all know of that at some point, mine probably also around that time too. But then my parents didn't abuse me (well the canes are a thing, but still, compared to her), nor did I not call for help through placing hints all over the place. I did not get invited to a birthday party whose birthday boy's mother's future death 18 years later should be more important than my death?? Oh well, now what? Okay, super summary aside (I think I did it bad, but whatever), with every episode we learn more. We now know that there are hints of a murderer on the loose, a creation of a time rift such that Kayo-chan cannot just die like that because she was invited to a party, and at this point - the transported-a-29-year-old-to-the-past version of time - the main guy and Kayo-chan know of despair. Let's just say that for now. Honestly saying? I think she is also under such a phenomenon. Because it's unlikely the mother will kill her. Maybe she is a psychopath for all we know, but hey, they never hinted that in the show. So, about Kayo-chan. She's probably following a trace of a murder about to happen, and if the phenomenon did NOT mention about tracing YOUR OWN DEATH... Yeah? You get where I'm coming from. I guess they both are searching for something. That's why the best way is to get to know each other.
P.S. Kajiura Yuki-sensei doing the music for this show makes things creepy. Remember Kara no Kyoukai?? Brr...

SHOW
MythBusters: IMPLODING TANK!!! I mean, cool in scale, and cool in physics. Well, theoretically not sound, but practically well done. Probably their largest take in an experiment, but also the most successful one, it seems. (I'm assuming they will show us their fails, but they didn't, so there must not have any) Going against everyone's opinions in the industry, the myth is proven to be busted, because the tankers can take up to vacuum pressure differentials even directly (like, pumping air out) and not bust inwards (or outwards, I guess). Of course they had to make it happen, but then you know, any tanker which had defects will already be decommissioned with any safety rules of using this thing already, so we're all safe. Good to know, and once again MythBusters has proven another point with science. Even though they probably would have a smaller budget if they had done a couple more smaller myths instead of this one. (Yes I'm complaining about the myth count this episode) Oh well, final season already, so I'm not complaining too much. Looking forward (if you watched the pre-season special), I'm waiting for the social myth episode. Remember the shopping myth? Hell yeah I love human science stuff.
