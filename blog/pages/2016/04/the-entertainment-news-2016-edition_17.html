Bungou Stray Dogs: Another episode. Hey, I'm impressed. By the animation, the characters, the story, everything. The story goes that this Atsushi-kun is given a chance to job at the Detective Agency (who surprisingly doesn't have an awesome name to it or something), and Dazai-stuck-in-a-oil-barrel-kun is here to give him said chance. The psychological mindf**king aside, Atsushi shows some instinct to save people around him, despite having a power of a were-tiger, apparently it's called. Nonetheless, this show is interesting, and I think I'll continue to watch it. We now know about half of the Agency, the President and that fake bomber included (which sister's voice sounds familiar but apparently I don't know her at all), and also the antagonist of the series. (I say "series" because this is Anime; I may be interested in the Manga though) This shall give some good insight to whether I want to continue watching this after all. Koufuku Graffiti: Finally someone picked it up like, last year, so this will be interesting. I saw something of Shiina-san with a confession? Man, I don't know what those people are posting about this Manga on Twitter, but I bet it's going to be good. I'm only on the first few chapters, and yes there are differences. Makoto-sensei's work has more details and background compared to the Anime itself (for example, how Kirin got fever because she's changing in and out of the trains with different temperatures), and yes it doesn't have the erotic-ness of eating compared to the adaptation. Hey, it's animation. What can I say? But I can say, the author's drawing is impeccable. So nice to watch, it feel as if I'm looking at those fake decorated food that are made to perfection. The comedy of the stories in the 4-panel manga stands out too, which makes it feel different in understanding compared to the show itself. I like it. I'll follow it till the end, provided the people doing the scans can too.
