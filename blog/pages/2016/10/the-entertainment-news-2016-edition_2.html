ANIME
WWW.WORKING!!: Wow, I'm amazed just this first episode (1).

Let's talk about how much this show is reminiscent of the original series. Well, you got a family restaurant with a bunch of weird people. And to whoever has watched the whole Anime series (including me) then this would come out as too much information to remember of most of the members introduced in this series. Otherwise, it'll just come out as weird as you'd probably need a long time to get used to the bunch of weird people (WTF?) in this workplace. Higashida has a solid foundation of why he works here, but I have a feeling the family is just in cahoots to convince him to work part-time, which will not be surprising after all. Miyakoshi-san is a strong yet funny woman to talk about to say the least, because if you think it's a prank for her to make chocolates I'm highly doubting about her cooking skills lol. Shiho-san (as I will refer to from now) is rich, we're not spoiled on why she is working in a restaurant anyways, but somehow she is the most materialistic woman I've seen in any Anime. I mean, she gives out money, man. That's gotta be something. And she pranks that poor guy Yuuta-kun without a change of face. Well that, I'll have you guys figure it out. Things are working out for that poor freak though, even though his current circumstances of having to work many jobs and taking leftovers from the food chain is not without reason. Sayuri-chan (because she's kinda cute) seems to be the most normal person in the shop, but then again as Higashida may have mentioned she has some eye for spiritual beings? Yeah, that's going to freak someone out. Adachi and Kendou are the guys in the kitchen, and they seem most normal for now, I guess. But hey, never judge a person by his cover, I would say. Kisaki-san is the last of the main staff (the manager later), and besides smoking her favourite past time is slacking off and making people think of her crazy knowledge she learnt from university. Problem is Higashida is as smart as her being a high school student so she's going to get to work somehow. The manager Sakaki seems to have a connection with Miyakoshi-san, as in being a target of violence. Hey, can't hurt with some cigarettes, violence and slapping of cash, right? This show got all the wrong stuff covered.
That's sincerity right there lol
(Where the f*** did Shiho-san learn this??)
All in all, I'm impressed. Even the opening theme song. It's fast-paced and real catchy. I think I'll grow to like this show a lot. Man Shiho-san please slam all the money you have on me please!! (I'm talking about Ten-chan here, but we can talk about that in future episodes xD) Anyways, this is something to watch if you liked the original, the weird stuff here are a lot more practical as it seems (I mean, I saw a katana somewhere in the ending) so it may be more to relate to...? Forget I said that. This show is just going to be a whole lot LOLs from here on.

Turns out it was all decoy and the real McCoy has been seeking guidance from the true ruler of the island Kairyuu-sama, but then again we only know of this much. That obviously is a lot more interesting than Ayane turning into I don't know what and fighting a fake, and whatever is happening in the real Japan that is a coincidence of whatever sorts. Let's hope it's not like an apocalypse thing where everyone wants to know of that mysterious island. Someone is feeling guilty as of right now...
