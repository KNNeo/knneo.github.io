アニメ
ANIME
Subete ga F ni Naru: Well many things happened, so let's dissect them out. After the director's death, they finally got into Magata-sensei's room, in which case is too clean to have a murder happen (due to a robot), and somewhat left a list of messages signalling her departure. As if that is not enough, we continue to dwelve into what Magata is about in person, having multiple personalities and her strong desire to leave the place, as hinted in this episode the title of the show, "where everything becomes F". This letter definitely doesn't stand for "Fail", or "Future", but can stand for "Futile" and anything else of my limited vocabulary can come up with. Nonetheless, they put that aside because everyone's tired (they still got the mood to sleep?) as we discuss deeper about Nishinosono's (I must say that's a great family name) past, with her parents, as this show may try to draw a parallel to give further (probably misleading) hints to the genius's death. Currently... I'm still buying the fact that it's all herself, and "she killed herself" to leave the place. I mean, who doesn't believe in the multiple personalities now that they showed it? And I swear she didn't have sex with her uncle. Just saying.

MANGA
Kuroshitsuji: After a long wait (in real time), it seems like... Ciel is trying to teach the green witch to treat herself as an asset. Of course I don't mean as a female (I mean look at the era; gender discrimination is still a thing, you know), but of her intelligence and not revealing her creation or research to as to protect the people of the world, or, as we have seen Sebastian do, leave it to the next generation of people who will probably be more responsible to handle such technology. Anyways, he gets haunted by Undertaker, which isn't all that surprising (so real I thought it's the last chapter already), but then again it's a hint. And I'm curious to know. Cue next chapter! Oh damn, I'll have to wait and see.
BLEACH: Well after 650 chapters we have confirmation of what Kubo has done for the zanpakuto soul arc back years ago on Anime before any of it ever appeared in the manga, and here it is. Names maybe different, but Kyoraku's zanpakuto soul definitely is a woman, and suicide by drowning apparently is (not) the way to go. Coming back to the fortunate times we see some happiness in the middle of plenty of chaos and action, the villian strikes back, probably making the Captain Commander a Hollow in the process. Will he die? Probably not. But is the bad guy dead? Of course not. As usual it'll take something epic to get rid of him (or not), but that's for next week.
