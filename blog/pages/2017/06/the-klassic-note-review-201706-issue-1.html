THE KLASSIC NOTE REVIEW
TrySail - adrenaline!!! [Single]
Limited EditionRegular EditionAnime Limited Edition
(I must say the colourful covers sure look nice, and the difference between Regular and Limited are minor, which is great)

Origin: I mean, we need no introductions anymore. Likely the reason to succeed in the idol music group that is sphere (which will pause all group activities this year), TrySail is also here to bring you great Anime tunes and with that, more interest to these three voice actresses, namely Natsukawa Shiina (in yellow; I have reviewed her solo debut single in April), Asakura Momo (in pink; Her new single is out soon) and Amamiya Sora (in blue; I've also reviewed her debut single). I may have stepped halfway in into the music careers of these three girls, but TrySail as a whole is pretty good this time round, enough to hold as competition to the other voice actor groups out there. I mean, I've mentioned about reviewing their first four singles too many times to show how far they have come in the past two years. The announcement of Anime "Eromanga-sensei" thus serves as an opportunity for them to show their best. And with this 7th 6th (the collaboration with HoneyWorks doesn't count, it seems) single fully in Anime tie-up category and them sounding this good, there is literally no reason why you would miss this.

(1) adrenaline!!!
I guess this song needs no introduction. At first impression, this Anime ending theme song is impressive. It's catchy, it's lively, and it has captured the 90 seconds perfectly, I couldn't even complain. Of course, when I got my hands on this song in full I did notice it isn't as colourful as I thought it would be. Still, this song is great. I couldn't say too much myself. Everything is added intentionally from the cheers to the highs in the song; everything in production is so well done. This is of course, the product of a single song composer and producer. Great song, and I don't see why anyone wouldn't like this. To top it off, the music video is even more reminiscent of the theme of TrySail which is sailing, and the hype in it is so high I'd rather not talk too much about fanservice. This is that typical awesome Anime theme song we've always wanted. Really, Ten-chan sounds soooo cuuuute in this song hehe
Rating: 4 stars (Above Average)

(2) Kakawari [Involvement]
Okay, not like I know of Madoka Magica and all, but this song is as impressive, if not better, than the ones that I started hearing of when this series started back in 2011. Yes, this might be initially a ClariS song. Familiar images surface when you start to hear the song in full, as many of the vibes and seriousness of this song brings you understanding from the Madoka Magica world and theme song style once again. No really, even the guy who produced the song is literally the one who has been writing all the theme songs for the series, which is in this case for a mobile game. Credits aside, this song also has its faults in the bridge part of the song, and catching up to the main melody of the chorus maybe a handful if your didn't get it the first time (which is what happened with me), but still. This song delivers great power and feelings towards the world of magic and despair to a certain extent, and is certainly a hear when you know you've been heard it for that much. The song, I mean.
Rating: 4 stars (Average)

Conclusion: Definitely one of the better singles I've heard in a while, mainly because it's so impressionable, and its nature of having Anime tie-ups means the songs will be good somehow (at least the choruses) and that's good enough. I can take two credited songs in one release. And you should take a listen too, considering that it's so good it's topping digital sales charts in Japan already. And with them moving towards an international audience soon with a change of music label company (SACRA MUSIC; as with ClariS and Kalafina too, amongst many others), we might just see them excel in the near future.

-Kaineng, on the Klassic Note
