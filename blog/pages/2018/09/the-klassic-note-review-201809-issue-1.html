THE KLASSIC NOTE REVIEW
Sambomaster - Kagayaki Dashite Hashitteku [Single]
(You know if anything else a cover art contains a school girl that it's a good cover hehe)

Origin: We should be familiar with Sambomaster, namely still the most famous Rock n' Roll band in Japan and still promoting and encouraging peoples' hearts all over the country. If you don't like them, you really need to give them a listen. Today with these guys, I always get excited, touched, and every other emotion in between. It's like an emotional rollercoaster every time with them. Today, they stand strong with 20 years of music experience behind their backs, and this one got through because of a specific television drama. And with it, comes the most touching work the trio have come up till date. Introducing the veteran, the legacy, and the always nostalgic future of Rock music in Klassic Note and their latest single, and you'll know why I didn't even hesitate to jump right into this one.

[*Note: There is no official listing of both the Limited Edition and Regular Edition singles but they do exist. It is implied that the Regular Ediiton single is a 1CD release which does not contain the live CD]

(1) Kagayaki Dashite Hashitteku [Running and Shining]
From the chorus:
"Don't give up your heart, let it shine on
It's okay, (you) will overcome
Don't be disheartened and please smile, bring up (your) chest and go
Because you, (are) sure to be my miracle"
Of course, the topic song. It was the topic for the television drama it was credited for (which is from a movie which talks about a jorney of a girl joining the cheerleader dancing group in school), and of course without much relation to it, I find hard to give them just the melody, so like every other Collection artist which I gave the thumbs up for, I always give a second chance. Diving into the lyrics did I know how much I felt for this song, considering when I heard this half a month ago I was still searching for a job. This song told me not to give up. This song taught me to overcome, be confident, and to presevere for I am a miracle to exist. Hey, with such lyrics, it's hard to not be touched. Coupled with a nostalgic yet fresh melody from these guys which I favour, you got yourself a successful song. Wonderful all around, and the lyrics will get you out of your latest rut, I promise.
Rating: 4 stars (Above Average)

(2) Dekikkonai wo Yara Nakucha [Have To Do (Something That I) Cannot Do]
From the chorus:
"Don't give up, regardless of when; you can do it, whatever it is
Now in this world (I) see the only strong force (in you)
Things that you cannot do, you can (actually) do it; don't lie
Now in this world (I) see the only strong light (in you)"
This was definitely not the deciding factor when it comes to giving this single mainly because this was already given. Credited as the insert song for the same drama mentioned above, this was actually a self cover of the original song back in 2010 when it was also credited for another show if I'm not wrong. Fast forward nearly 8 years into this release, and this song it still great. The melody caught my attention back then almost immediately, and the lyrics - after much learning of Japanese back then - brought much meaning. Again, around the premises of never giving up no matter what, this one brings the never-die spirit and straightforward singing into a new level. Forget about songs which bring subtle meaning or even no meaning; Those with meaning will bring out the best in them, especially songs like these. And this one, the one sung in many lives and loved by the fans, is definitely the first song I'll introduce you to so that you'll love Sambomaster as much as I do. What may be slightly different (I suppose they bought the license for this song since they switched music labels) is in the rendition of the instrumentals since this was actually re-done, so maybe not as awesome as the original. Other than that, it's a treasure.
Rating: 4 stars (Above Above Average)

Conclusion: I couldn't say much about giving this single, but if you liked the topic song then you'll love this single, I assure you. As a long time Sambomaster fan, I've always appreciated their efforts for making simple melodies which have heavy meaning in their lyrics, and I would say this is a true example of their contributions. Give them a listen to if you haven't, and maybe, just maybe, it'll touch your heart too. Turn away, and you may never have a chance to be touched by music again.


- Kaineng, on the Klassic Note
