アニメ Tada-kun wa Koi wo Shinai
Oh come on Charles you got nothing bad to say AT ALL??? I mean, we're talking about Anime characters at least show some disrespect haha
The new episode (#TadaKun06) brings us a big question: is there anything related between Teresa-chan and Tada-kun??? Okay, chances that there aren't, but I can't help but think about it. The new episode brings us Charles, chances are he's of royal standards too, he's the perfect man, and he's a candidate to be married to the future Queen of the nation. Yes, talking about you, Teresa-chan. I guess the Anime didn't seem to make of much impact (I mean, the most impact I got was from Alec-san which I can understand she has a huge responsibility on her hands) to bring out the idea of two things: the importance of Teresa having to make a decision of why she was in Japan and how this ties to her royal marriage, and how much the main couple are interested in each other whether through coincidence or interests. I don't know. I felt that it was weak. And I personally don't think smuggling those kids into a rich man's party will make it any easier for story-telling tbh. But at least something was clear at the end of the episode, and that is Teresa-chan is future Queen, she needs a husband, and Charles shall be it. Maybe. I mean, no other options? The problem is, we don't know. Unless we'd like to think that Tada-kun has ancestors tied to the Larsenberg royal family which is totally unlikely, there doesn't seem to have any easy solution to say that her future is not sealed. Which means this show is going to do it the hard way - talking us through romance. And you bet that's gonna be fun! I mean, up till now we haven't really gotten anything from them that is considered romantic (I mean, the jacket thing is just manners imo) so we don't know what any of the stuff in this episode means yet. I guess an action speaks a thousand words (*wink* Tsuki ga Kirei), but I guess it's not enough. But hey, we are progressing. Halfway through the series already, and I have hopes that this story can still surprise me. I'll be looking forward to that.

漫画 Amaetai Hi wa Soba ni Ite.
Wow that took some time to read lol. The 1st volume of Kawai Makoto-sensei's book is out, and I finally got a chance to read it. (Someday, sensei, I'll buy your boob books) Since it's all in Japanese, I'll summarise the story for you guys to garner some interest. In this world, there are humans. And there are androids. Who look like humans and benefit the society by serving as a friendly alternative to human professionals and machinery, especially in the service industry. Hinageshi is one of those ladies (I suppose there are males too, but) who is assigned to the maid part of the industry after receiving some level of training. The closest resemblance I can give you on this is maid employment in developed countries, but this is technically not human. She services a young student and manga author Kaede (this one's a him) who suffered from a recent incident following the death of both his parents. Yeah, sad story as usual, and Hinageshi kind of did her part to help him and cheer him up, so that's good news. Then again, Kaede is not alone, for he has acquaintances from the company he has to submit his work to, and there's the childhood friend Azuki, who probably is fond of him but the blockhead can't take any hints so whatever. (Up till here, think Anime Sangatsu no Lion's Kiriyama and his emotions and without shougi and the Kawamoto sisters.) Everything is well in the house, but there's one catch: Hinageshi has a crush on Kaede. It's not about societal hierarchy or race though, but because there's the one law which states that androids are not allowed to develop romantic relationships with humans. And androids technically still can. They just can't. That's the story.
Hinageshi, the android
(Yes I'm totally trying to sell her image here hehe)
Of course, it isn't that simple. There's one thing that will make this public, following heavy consequences in public shame and disrespect for androids if this isn't dealt with discretely or properly, as case studies show. The one thing, is that Hinageshi and Azuki can't keep thoughts to themselves. That's Anime level of honestly right there lol. So after finding out that Hinageshi has feelings for her owner, Azuki can't help but be stuck in a decision of whether to let her be or not. That she didn't decide, but she did discuss with her friend and the friend's house android. Yeah. That's probably where the problem starts to sprout. Hinageshi then gets a call of notice with a deadline of two weeks from a mysterious person to make a decision to do this discretely and avoid further problems. And guess what? Of course she denied. (Backstory required which I will not elaborate here) Even if it's from the senpai who has been taking care of her since the days as an android trainee. Yeah, someone's gotta be the bad guy here. But it doesn't stop there, and I don't think I should spoil any further.
Azuki, the childhood friend
(Part-time stalker lol)
This is turning out to be more interesting than I thought. The first chapter gave me a bad impression of making me think a lot for androids, less for feelings, and it's just another love story. But man was I wrong. Within all the minuscule eyebrow twitching and emotion-sniffing (lol), huge racks (sorry Azuki-chan) and nonchalant faces (well there's only that one guy) is actually quite some depth in story to substantiate the end of this volume, which is the android decision to help the human along his life, see him grow up, and (hopefully) not fall in love because it's against the law. We shall see. Now that things have escalated to a new level, I can't help but think this might affect Kaede's reputation as a manga author. I don't know, Makoto-sensei. Blow my mind.

音楽 This Week on Klassic Note
Well next week's gonna be rough so I'll give first impressions now before my mood swings and I can't give any more new songs lol

This week brings us quite a lot of releases, some of them may have not even appeared which is a problem, but hey, let's talk about those that did appear.
