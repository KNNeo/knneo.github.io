ã‚¢ãƒ‹ãƒ¡ Yoru wa Mijikashi, Aruke yo Otome
[(The) Night Is Short, Walk On Girl]
And the least I'd expect is to hear Kana-chan speak fast. Hey, welcome to the Morimi Torihiko world, ladies and gentlemen.
Oh damn am I exited to jump into this same world as Anime Yojouhan Shinwa Taikei. And boy was it an amazing production. I swear, it brings back memories, and then some. I find myself enjoying this movie to the fullest, all wrapped up in a story that happens in a single night.

Let's talk about story for a bit, because unlike the Anime, this movie "actually as a plot here". Plot goes that the senpai (main guy) was finding a way to be with the girl he likes, the girl with the black hair (main girl). And so, in her night of craze besides sticking by a normal wedding night, she starts a tour of the city and gets involved in everything she is in. The thing is, she finds amazement in everything she does, never leaving regret behind otherwise not drawn by fate. But she forgot something. That fate can be created. By someone. Who has an intention. I'm not saying it's a bad thing, but hey, what's wrong with chasing a girl?

This show summarises my life so well, I don't even know how to comment. (Okay, besides the drinking, the hotplate deathmatch, and I had someone to love in university haha) Firstly, let's talk about production. This wonderful movie is done in the exact same way as the Anime I've seen five years ago, and boy am I glad to have watched that screw up of a show. But then, it left an impression I wasn't able to forget, and a lesson I'll always remember. (Be sure to watch that first before watching this) Cue in the announcement for this movie to be done in the exact same way but featuring a different story written by the same author, my anticipation for this went to an all time high. Featuring some familiar voices (Hanazawa Kana *chu*, Kamiya Hiroshi who also is very similar in popularity to his acting character lol), I can't wait for this to come out. And I'm not disappointed. Watching this type of animation is like going on a journey, and I might say, comparable to Studio Ghibli movies. Add in a nice touch of unexpected musical format to keep the format short yet eventful (because they don't have 11 episodes to do never-ending speeches like last time), the journey was fun. Just like the girl with the black hair, we had moved forward consistently without having to worry about devotion and just to enjoy life to the fullest. And that is its beauty.

Then, there's the references. I don't know about old songs which the musical might have adapted from to tell the story in the guerrilla theatre, the show is FULL of Yojouhan references. From familiar faces (Higuchi, the chairman, Jougasaki lol) to even more familiar scenarios depicted from speech to visuals (lol boob wall), because this was adapted from a similar environment which is the university that the main guy of the Yojouhan Anime was from, I felt right at home the moment it started. I was mostly laughing because I've became the target of the many jokes from the very familiar yet ironic references in the movie, I can only but laugh.

Finally, the pace. In this types of animation, most appreciated and the most underappreciated must be its pace. It's so fast, you thought the night wind in the movie that was giving everyone the colds (I mean, it's technically a typhoon) was blowing faster than the plot. And then they even reminded you with it in various ways, from the clocks/watches of the elderly running faster than the young main characters (reflection of society btw) to how all this happens in one night (if you didn't catch it the main girl said it in visit to Rihaku's house when he was ill) to Johnny's appearance in the senpai causing in an emergency, and despite all that, it did not miss on storytelling. I can guarantee you that after watching this, it's either you get all of it or you get none of it. Well, it may be because of the references, but still. It's a hell of a ride of freedom. And it all makes sense from a book ownership perspective, just like how the God of Used Books has desired. Now we can all rest well, knowing we've yet witnessed another wonderful romance tale that will never exist in our bland, empty and meaningless lives, just like the Fake Denki Bran hehe.
