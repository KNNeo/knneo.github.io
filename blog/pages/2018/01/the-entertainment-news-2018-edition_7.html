And the least I'd expect is to hear Kana-chan speak fast. Hey, welcome to the Morimi Torihiko world, ladies and gentlemen.
Oh damn am I exited to jump into this same world as Anime Yojouhan ShinwaTaikei. And boy was it an amazing production. I swear, it brings back memories,and then some. I find myself enjoying this movie to the fullest, all wrapped upin a story that happens in a single night.

Let's talk about story fora bit, because unlike the Anime, this movie "actually as a plot here". Plot goesthat the senpai (main guy) was finding a way to be with the girl he likes, thegirl with the black hair (main girl). And so, in her night of craze besidessticking by a normal wedding night, she starts a tour of the city and getsinvolved in everything she is in. The thing is, she finds amazement ineverything she does, never leaving regret behind otherwise not drawn by fate.But she forgot something. That fate can be created. By someone. Who has anintention. I'm not saying it's a bad thing, but hey, what's wrong with chasing agirl?

This show summarises my life so well, I don't even know how tocomment. (Okay, besides the drinking, the hotplate deathmatch, and I had someoneto love in university haha) Firstly, let's talk about production. This wonderfulmovie is done in the exact same way as the Anime I've seen five years ago, andboy am I glad to have watched that screw up of a show. But then, it left animpression I wasn't able to forget, and a lesson I'll always remember. (Be sureto watch that first before watching this) Cue in the announcement for this movieto be done in the exact same way but featuring a different story written by thesame author, my anticipation for this went to an all time high. Featuring somefamiliar voices (Hanazawa Kana *chu*, Kamiya Hiroshi who also is very similar inpopularity to his acting character lol), I can't wait for this to come out. AndI'm not disappointed. Watching this type of animation is like going on ajourney, and I might say, comparable to Studio Ghibli movies. Add in a nicetouch of unexpected musical format to keep the format short yet eventful(because they don't have 11 episodes to do never-ending speeches like lasttime), the journey was fun. Just like the girl with the black hair, we had movedforward consistently without having to worry about devotion and just to enjoylife to the fullest. And that is its beauty.

Then, there's thereferences. I don't know about old songs which the musical might have adaptedfrom to tell the story in the guerrilla theatre, the show is FULL of Yojouhanreferences. From familiar faces (Higuchi, the chairman, Jougasaki lol) to evenmore familiar scenarios depicted from speech to visuals (lol boob wall), becausethis was adapted from a similar environment which is the university that themain guy of the Yojouhan Anime was from, I felt right at home the moment itstarted. I was mostly laughing because I've became the target of the many jokesfrom the very familiar yet ironic references in the movie, I can only butlaugh.

Finally, the pace. In this types of animation, mostappreciated and the most underappreciated must be its pace. It's so fast, youthought the night wind in the movie that was giving everyone the colds (I mean,it's technically a typhoon) was blowing faster than the plot. And then they evenreminded you with it in various ways, from the clocks/watches of the elderlyrunning faster than the young main characters (reflection of society btw) to howall this happens in one night (if you didn't catch it the main girl said it invisit to Rihaku's house when he was ill) to Johnny's appearance in the senpaicausing in an emergency, and despite all that, it did not miss on storytelling.I can guarantee you that after watching this, it's either you get all of it oryou get none of it. Well, it may be because of the references, but still. It's ahell of a ride of freedom. And it all makes sense from a book ownershipperspective, just like how the God of Used Books has desired.
