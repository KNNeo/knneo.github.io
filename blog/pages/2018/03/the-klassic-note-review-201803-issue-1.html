THE KLASSIC NOTE REVIEW
TrySail - WANTED GIRL [Single]
Limited EditionRegular EditionAnime Limited Edition
(Ignoring the Anime cover art, feels and looks like they're doing a spy movie parody, and it looks pretty good. Gotta love that Nan-chan butt lol)

Origin: I feel like I shouldn't do much of an introduction since I've not failed to review every single one of their singles till date including this, but hey, you should know them by now. They sing in all the latest Anime you (might) watch, and recently they announced another single which will be out in June (I probably will be reviewing it too if all goes as well as this one), they cute, they sing well... you get the idea. If the likes of Amamiya Sora (Limited Edition cover, in blue), Asakura Momo (in red) and Natsukawa Shiina (in yellow) can't cater to any of your idolism tastes, then I'm sorry. Otherwise you'll be glad to know this voice actress-led idol group has been doing well, and as of now on their journey to their 3rd album. This time, they sing for a... I would say, "show targeted at younger kids" mainly because I don't watch it (lol and I watched PriPri Chii-chan) (for three seasons straight) but hey, it doesn't stop them from having a good song. And this single may just prove that. Something I couldn't miss out, as usual.

(1) WANTED GIRL
This feels like some dangerous song, something exciting, something lively... and that's what the song gives in terms of the mood. And yes, I'm hooked into it the moment I hear it. The rest of the song kind of falls in well so I won't complain, the lyrics can be quite a mouthful (it's been two week and I still can't sing to the words) (and I can be considered a pro already), but other than those this song is surprisingly good, and I'm liking it. This is the kind of song you'll like the more you listen to it, and honestly I may be hooked. Still better than being tied up, if you did watch the music video haha poor Mocho
Rating: 4 stars (Average)

(2) Sanpomichi [Trail]
As usual, this is the deal breaker. But I won't say it is so, because my reason for it is "I just don't want to give this song in the future", and I think you may recall my experience with this two times already with three singles, so yeah, I'm future-proofing Klassic Note as you can see here. Then again, this three-beat tempo isn't bad on the ears, it feels very nostalgic to their earlier B-side songs which are pretty easy-listening so I decided to give it. The usual rant can come from a kind of unnatural bridge part of the song but that's okay I gave it anyway. I just feel more glued to the melody and the overall feel of the tempo. I mean, you don't get three-beat tempo songs every time, even though when they appear they usually are good. (ðŸ˜‰ Kalafina) ("Not anymore they aren't" Ouch) So yeah, I felt a beat when I hear these kinds of songs, and for TrySail at least it's hitting on me, and I like that.
Rating: 4 stars (Above Average)

Conclusion: Not a bad single, and probably still close to one of their best, may be overwhelmed by the B-side, but the topic song is also good since the credit probably (this is an Anime thing now) gives more freedom to the composition of the song, so yeah we get a good single. Would not really recommend you to listen to it if you didn't like their single B-side styles, but yeah, this single is pretty easy to give even at first listen.

-Kaineng, on the Klassic Note
