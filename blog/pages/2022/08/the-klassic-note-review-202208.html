Ishihara Kaori - Abracada-Boo [Single]
Limited Edition Regular Edition
(I mean, I wish it wasn't an Anime cover for the Regular Edition but heck, it has a huge rack of I don't know who so I'll take it)

Origin: The origins of this particular single might be more of a coincidence than just pure idolism at play, let me explain. I was initially going to watch the Anime behind the other single "Isekai Pharmacy" which was released on the same date by this voice actress Ishihara Kaori but did not watch because I hit my own quota, but that did not stop me getting interested in this double single release since I knew of her from last year. Not double A-side, not album, two singles released in one day, in view of her birthday that week. I was then naturally attracted to the topic song of the other single which is this one, and things sort of went haywire. It only got better so I did not hesitate to give this at all. The slight awakening is just part of the illusion. Let's talk about the magic involved in this.

(1) Abracada-Boo
From the first chorus:
"Even if the world condemns (you) as a crime
Even (you) being alone
(I) want to continue to understand you
I'm on your side to protect you"
This was also an Anime theme song along with the other single which already shows how much Kyari-san here is wanted for her singing talent apparently, as this is just as cool of a song to listen to as it is to watch the music video which is just a total fanservice since you get to see her dance which would capture most of her audience. What also did capture all of us though, is that the catchy melody is not shy in expressing itself, and along with the lyrics portraying I don't know if it's for the Anime, protecting someone with the power of magic which of course we can find similarities with idolism in general, so overall this is too good a package to miss. What I would complain about is how Kyari-san can't really catch up with the singing in some places in this recorded version, and maybe a longer than expected bridge if we are talking about a cooler piece it would be good to be shorter. But nothing is short about this being bad, I can tell you that much.
Rating: Magically Above Average

(2) Aimai Shinkirou
From the first chorus:
"The days (I) love, love, love, love, burying the gaps (of time)
(The) night view (is) shining, shining, shining, shining, too much it's troubling
It's as if, (this) is a mirage
(I'm) sure I've spent those days (with you), (I'm) pretty sure..."
And who doesn't want a love song to couple with a cool song? It's emotional, it's cute, and it's lovely beyond description. This might have been my first impression when I listened to the teaser short on her YouTube channel. Curiosity got to the better of me, and when she slaps in the "love" in the chorus ironically talking about the days spent with, you guessed it, and I already loved the song. The mood of the song isn't exactly emotional by any means though, but the lyrics will make sure you miss the days being with your loved ones, too great you thought it wasn't real. The song itself might be dragging itself out with the mediocre melody, but the lyrics carry much meaning I wish I could translate the whole song for all to see. For that, I think I will love this song more than the artist singing behind it, for time to come.
Rating: Lovingly Average

Conclusion: I may have complimented too much on it, but above all else this is a very typical Anime theme song release draped with the best of Ishihara Kaori's image and style, I could have easily picked this up even without knowing about her through Anime. And so I did, and I'm still glad I've heard of the potential of Kyari-san herself apart from what I knew of her a year ago. I will only continue to "love" this single, even if she doesn't remind me of it through her singing. For the conventional listener, the lyrics are also straightforwardly meaningful, so if you want just something great to listen to, this might just be it.


(Seriously though, I see many familiar names from this PONY CANYON release and goddamn did they put the same people who impressed me before to make this such a wonderful release. I particularly thank RIRIKO-sensei [c/w Ueda Reina for Anime "MajoTabi"] and Fujibayashi Shouko [c/w Taketatsu Ayana] for the perfectly tuned lyrics so I can love Ishihara Kaori more. Love letter over haha)

Asakura Momo - Stained Glass [Single]
Limited Edition Regular Edition
(Yes, close-ups are better, thanks for asking)

Origin: We're back with more love(ly) songs from voice actress Asakura Momo, member of Anime idol group TrySail, and we have been five years into her solo music career. Fuelled by love songs and sometimes Anime if we have the chance, her cute voice only accents the wonderful melodies that come with it, and since her 2nd album back in 2020, music production have been exploring and coming up with different styles and presentation for such a cute voice to fit in. With the 3rd album months after this single though did I then actually give the B-side in order for me to talk about this, so we are already late to the party. But I will talk more about the artistic theme of this though, for this is not your usual fare.

(1) Stained Glass
From the first chorus:
"Shining bright light into the bright sky
(I'm) jumping, playing, (in) happiness
Savour this moment all the way, please
Feels like what (you were) trying to convey

Now (my) heart is increasing in flutter, and stained
(Your) sourness and sweetness (I) love them all
Beautiful happiness in pieces
The times (I) love when (you) give (me) colour"
How should I start... my first impression should be the grand entrance of the instrumentals in the chorus, presenting itself as an impactful and bursting out freedom-like experience, sort of a presentation of a free soul, and this is what the essence of this song is about too. And that is along with the lyrics which is describing stained glass in general but also adding the idea of a feeling of a freely moving soul, if that makes any sense to you. These made the impression of this song more unique than I have heard in years, and thinking about it now, this will truly live to make me remember how much I liked the melody of this otherwise unbalanced feel of freedom. The music video is also adequately themed to the object in question.
Rating: Marvellously Above Average

(2) Nemui Kedo [But (I'm) Tired]
From the first verse:
"Though (I'm) tired, (I'm) tired; (but) going out is priority
Though (I'm) tired, (I'm) tired; (but) the smell outdoors, (I'm) keen
Movies, lunch or yawns (I) won't compromise on
Because (I am) with you"
Well well, we have come back to love song territory again, but before that, I should talk about why my return to this song. My first impression of this was more of cheap repeated lyrics and a heavy pop theme, and that really deterred me off when I was listening to a pop heavy week of releases back then so this did not stand out. But listening to it on the album again this is way more palatable compared to the more innovative design in melodies for the other songs in the release given its simpler feel, and that is what gave me the second chance to this tune. And of course, the simple lyrics are probably the highlight of this song itself, as once again, someone has written them to make sure she will come to you and let you be her arm pillow. What else do you need me to describe? I feel like I'm already tired telling you about this song; You should experience it for yourself.
Rating: Exhaustingly Average

Conclusion: I don't know how to describe this single. It has all the songs I would want in a single, yet I don't think I would say this has a theme strong enough to make most people like this release in particular. The topic song is a theme itself, but it has a B-side which is typical Mocho fare, so unless you want to know something from her solo music career while still be able to hear something different, then you have come to the right place. The album brought me into this review, so don't ask how I missed this instead of diving into more unconventional presentation styles in her album afterwards. I won't question too much on how good this single feels now in my opinion, though.

- Aozaki Shouhei, on the Klassic Note
