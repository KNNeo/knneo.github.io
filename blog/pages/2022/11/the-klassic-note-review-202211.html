Natsukawa Shiina - Sasakure [Single]
Limited Edition Regular Edition
(Yes, thanks for the close-ups and the garden themed cover with all the soil for some reason and getting her dirty lol)

Origin: This year didn't have enough emotional songs, so I guess TrySail youngest member Natsukawa Shiina decided to sing a sad song which was first released on her birthday and single releasing on end September... until it was delayed till November. Things may not have gone well out there, but what is inside this new single feels somewhat like a repeat of what we have experienced time and again, with impact and with emotion, and maybe sometimes a little cheeky, which might be her best single yet. With her on the lyrics, she expresses a different or perhaps real self to show us the true form of her solo music career.

(1) Sasakure [Splinter]
From first chorus:
"If (I) pretend to touch your core
Can you escape your boring existence from yesterday?
I won't say anything, just that maybe I'm wrong
That's a ridiculous statement, isn't it? (Just) laugh"
Wow, how should I start... It all started with her birthday present to us fans, which means this song has been released in full about 4 months ago, and bearing similarities to her 3rd single topic song, this song is as real as is impactful in melody. Maybe it's me being weak to songs with huge contrast in emotion, and this one carries the can. From the drop of the melody into the chorus, to her own lyrics composition about feeling unsatisfied just going along with life... It might have said a lot to some of us right here and now. All this only makes my impression of her with all the more respectful, and really sheds a light on how little I can complain about even the melody she was not in charge of. For this, the impression of this song might get stuck in me, for better or for worse.
Rating: Impressively Above Average

(2) passable :(
From first chorus:
"There's nothing wrong in (us) the contents
Then why is there no growth?
There's nothing wrong in (us) the contents
Being "average" is like this
There's nothing wrong in (us) the contents
When the feels are "fair"
Being so hollow (inside, we) feel balance
In other words, until then (there is no growth); I know I know"
As always, it's the B-side which made this a review to talk about, and contrary to the topic song, this is a simple melody from when I first heard of it on the archive of her late night Monday radio show, and then realized this is too good of a Rock tune and so chill of a tempo I don't have much to talk about. Even the bridge is reasonably short and concise, it's good. The lyrics written by yours truly talk about, as the title would let you think, about just being average and while that doesn't just mean being childish, it can say true for the many of us youngsters and how we think today. And I think that's just a plus sign for what she speaks out of her mind when writing the words for this song. Add in a little quirky cuteness throughout the songs, "I know", this is already a song to remember, "I know".
Rating: Chill Average


Conclusion: I can't say better things about this single. This was also how I felt walking into these two songs in this release. And I feel like my respect for dear Natsukawa Shiina only deepens the more I understand her from writing lyrics, and it is only hitting deeper into the soul. Overall though, I feel like these two songs easily represent her solo music career, where they allow her to sing emotional songs with impact while still being able to sing standard fare Rock songs and still excel at them. To this, I can only appreciate more of what is shown to me more than whatever ended the B-side with her jovial laugh put into this. That, I think this deserves more attention even for mainstream, to hear what she has to say through music.

- Aozaki Shouhei, on the Klassic Note
