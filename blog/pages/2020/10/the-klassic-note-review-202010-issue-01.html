音楽 The Klassic Note Review
Natsukawa Shiina - Antithesis [Single]
Limited Edition
 Regular Edition
(Don't ask me why let her hold the traffic post which is real heavy according to her, but I'll take the slight closer-up, thank you)

Origin: This, is a single of sorts. This is a single from her own music label and talent company MusicRay'n moving into the new age, in my opinion. It was April 2020, a time of confusion and instability, and that's where Natsukawa Shiina - voice actress and member of unit group TrySail - started her own YouTube channel. And first in her group. In the whole company, even. I suppose it was probably then where she covered songs she liked from rap to modern pop rock tunes that led to this single, I might say. Fast forward a couple of months later, she introduced her new single to us, bringing up much hype to every aspect of it, from the songs, to the music video, behind-the-scenes footage and so on... all produced/edited by yours truly, this is truly an age to live in. I hope that she really enjoyed making this single as much as I listened to it, because I tell you, this is some kind of a release.

(1) Antithesis
From the first chorus:
"Always
Until (my) emotion limit can be released, I'm still holding the grudge to be alone
Above this kind of life and still eating away, (I'm) still strongly, strongly struggling"
If this is not the definition of modern day pop rock, I can't find another description. Listening to this within seconds it'll hook you in, as with the intention of the indie composer and producer Surii would intend to do, and even later presented in a lyric video about how rebellious this song might sound, and it's totally on point. The song and music video show the strong and cool type image of Nan-chan we might otherwise not see (all but more, of course), but this makes it even cooler, embodying itself into a song that dabs itself. With a catchy melody and lyrics to remember, this will make a point across, with hype and even fit with anger. So much power, you'll won't even question my criticism of parts of this song I would otherwise dislike, just because.
Rating: 4 stars (Crazily Above Average)

(2) RUNNY NOSE
From the first chorus:
"(I) want to stand out, (I) want to laugh (it out)
(I) want to be in a form without keeping up image
(I) want to expose (myself) but it still hurts
Peeling off (my) shell
(I still have to) Come up with a reckless excuse"
And as if one hype song isn't enough, the B-side will make you never put down this release. Apart from the fact that the solo artist singer herself came up with the lyrics which are hard to read even if I have read Japanese for the past decade, the music itself will be enough for you to bop your head, let alone shake it like it's Saturday night. It's also this that brought in a great first impression when she revealed this on her YouTube channel, just days before release date. The whole song itself is even more impressive than I thought, though I did hesitate in giving the song mostly because it'll take some time to get used to, considering the genre and obvious extension of melody to keep up with the lyrics. As much as this is a weakness of having separate composer and lyricist, this won't stop you from cheering with the background vocals, and shaking your head up and down in the chorus. This truly is a wild song inside out.
Rating: 4 stars (Rocking High on Average)

Conclusion: Everything done here might be an experiment. To suit Shiina's tastes, to test out modern day social media, to go into a genre the company itself hardly explores... all in the name of an emergency. I guess that's how we will have something good in our hands right now. If you are a fan of her, I'm not going to convince you to do otherwise, but if you have heard any of the songs on YouTube and thought this was cool? You're only hitting the tip of the iceberg, my friend. Nan-chan can do so much more, you'll think her cuteness is getting her way, and I love her efforts for that. This single will go down as something to remember, even for her as an solo artist.

- Kaineng, on the Klassic Note
