THE INTERVIEW
As with most photobooks I have reviewed, there is an interview in this one because why not know more about her in paragraphs of Japanese text which I have to translate a summary to you guys after spending hours understanding what was written? Here we go then, is a list of the important points you need to know about Akarin and the photo shoot itself provided she is not as long-winded as Anzai Chika haha:
  • Kitou Akari was born 16th October 1994; her name came about from her parents wanting her to be cheerfully brought up and specifically her dad wants her to shine in everything she does, and this the name "Aka-ri" was formed. Akarin said her as a kid is one who somehow is not afraid to chat up to strangers. She listed an example where her mother would be shopping at the supermarket and she'll be talking to some random old man on the street. (lol really)
  • Akarin went through a chuunibyou (ie. rebellious) phase too but in the form of appearance. Around the end of elementary school she began to dress up in more gentlemanly outfits including pants and keeping her hair short and wearing black shirts with skulls.
  • She is probably influenced a lot from manga and/or anime, stating that she reads a lot of shounen manga and watched a lot of shows back then. (As a female you'll probably prefer shoujo genres, where it's those lovey-dovey prince saves princess plots, not the male-preferred shounen genres where action and harem genres take the lead)
  • She has a younger sister (hello sister-in-law, haha), and his dad would spoil them by buying manga for them to read because he's a big fan of them himself. Normally he would buy the first volume for Akarin and say "this is rather interesting" and then she will end up having to buy the rest herself. (lol the dad is like a living advertisement God Akarin I love your father already oh hi dad haha)
  • Her favourites in manga include (get ready weebs) "HUNTER x HUNTER", "BLEACH" (Yo~!! haha), "D.Gray-Man", and specifically likes more of "Air Gear". (Feel free to find out about these series which were popular a decade ago lol)
  • Middle school Akarin was hooked into the Internet. (lol who doesn't? FYI that's around 2005-2006) She would always browse the Internet by borrowing her dad's PC and (I asssume laptop because) she would also learn her illustrations on them.
  • She decided on becoming a voice actress only close to graduation during her high school days. This was when she had to decide on her future path, but her dad dropped the bomb on her and said "get your own money or a scholarship to get into college (university)" and did not provide her the funds to do so because he doesn't have them. (Hey, no one said their family was rich the dad spent it on manga) (Haha, a true manga fan) So that was when Akarin had to make a different choice in education, and she chose to pursue her dream of being a voice actress as a way to allow her to complete her education, so she gave up on that for this dream. (Aw...) She did mention that she would like to challenge herself (to go to college) if she could in the future. (Oh it's those Kotobuki Minako 1-year hiatus again lol)
  • So in the end, the parents did not give her allowance for a year, she worked for half a year part-time but could not get nearly enough funds to do anything (probably enough to go by tbh), but it was also then her talent company Pro Fit was recruiting members biannually and so the parents allowed her to "take this challenge for once" with tears in their eyes. She eventually passed, and got into the company itself. (Again, a true genius haha)
  • She says her parents really treat her with respect on the decisions she makes in life. They had the "do it with your own efforts" kind of mindset. Even when she was part-timing as an illustrator and got recommended by friends online to meet up outside town and stuff, her parents would be "if you can pay for your own expenses you can go" and she did. (The best kind of parents) She felt that is how she ended up paving the "route" of life for herself. She said if not for such parents and they would give her all the money she would just do things without much thought. (Yeah you looking at a live example right here, but then again I think it's also me being a pussy haha)
  • Since she started getting work as soon as she entered her talent company, her student life soon became empty as it was filled with work. (Refer to Kohara Rin, from "Sore ga Seiyuu!") And as soon as she graduated, she had to go into city (ie. Tokyo), so in the process she lost a lot of friends and felt the pains of having to meet new people in another society. Since she was not confident in her skills as a voice actor, her fear of the adults around her only grew stronger. Because she can technically not listen to adults in school ie. teachers and get away with it, but if you go against adults out there you'll be done for. This is what made her shrink even further in the face of society.
  • Her first Anime role back in 2014 was in "Rokujouma no Shinryakusha!?" (as Aika Maki, supporting character), and as one of the villians to the main character, she got a solid two episodes worth of dialogue, and she felt that it was hard. Since she got her role real quick, she didn't have time to familiarise herself with the studio, so she had to learn everything from scratch, which got her real nervous at the time. She was in the "fear of adults" feels too, so she said she could barely remember anything from it. (Haha man that is rough) She was living in anticipation of this recording to end as soon as possible, sort of attitude.
  • Her break from being... afraid of adults came in 2016 from Anime "Boku Dake ga Inai Machi" (ie. "ERASED" because I did watch that, really don't know she was in it though) (I mean, there was Yuuki Aoi-chan haha), and in "Time Bokan 24" as one of the two main roles, and it was because she was practically surrounded by veterans that she started to learn a lot from them. She felt that these provided a good step up into overcoming that fear.
  • On asking what she treasured in her voice actress career, (I don't think she is answering the question lol, but) she said she was the type that likes to think a lot about her work. Even in school with math she can think all day about the equations and in the test, and yet in the end she still could not get it anyways.
  • She was serious about her work, but sometimes the production would give her directions on how to do it but she still would not get as to why, and practised too much from overthinking such things. Because she felt that inspiration is vital to expression of voices in Anime (quite true), but she did follow the directions told to her accordingly and learnt how to adapt to the recording which in turn increased her confidence on the job.
  • She would like to work on different roles that would last. (I assume like, from series with sequels, say "KonoSuba") Also, roles that were thought to be perfect for her were also preferred by her.
