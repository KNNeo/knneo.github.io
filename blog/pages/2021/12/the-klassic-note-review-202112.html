音楽 The Klassic Note Review
Kitri - Hikare Inochi [Single]
(That's all to be honest; It's not like we know if Komi-san knows how to play the piano)

Origin: I think it comes off as surprise to me as it is to them, but this sisters singer-songwriter group is doing pretty well for themselves. Pretty much all of the songs are written by them, they had to let a producer in to do his work for Anime ending theme of "Komi-san", but that doesn't mean they made it worse, because as we have learnt that's how they get their styles in, and as we will talk about in detail, you'll realize they have the same vibes as if you have missed hearing them a decade ago even though they are totally new to the industry. Let's see what is in them in an age full of image that makes their music stand out.

(1) Hikare Inochi [Charmed Life]
From first chorus:
"Charmed (by) life
Shouting from within the heart
Whenever the hardship might be too
"The sky is nice" and so on, those nice words
And move forward with (those)"
I have to say that I was pretty impressed with this song at first listen, and being an Anime theme song does have its benefits, but more than that, we have overlooked on what was inside it that makes it good. For starters, it is a wonderful ending song, and it always hits the heart every week, so you can understand how nice this is already in impact. But more than that, since the girls wrote the lyrics, they also knew to write them to suit the main character in the Anime who is in essence a very fresh but yet nice person, which will explain her desires to break out of her social anxiety to make friends, in her case. In this song though, the lyrics bring more of hope and motivation of life itself, and how we are stronger being with others. For these, I'm proud to have given this song.
Rating: 4 stars (Better than normal Anime theme songs Average)

(2) Sympathy
From first verse:
Cool person? Nice person?
The type to hold (a) secret?
Making judgements based on image is such a taboo
Before (the) brain decides to befriend or otherwise
Let's try to talk (to each other)"
Ah, this felt like such a joke because of its use in Episode 1 of the Anime until you realize its potential. At first listen, this didn't hit me more than a meme because it was literally used as a drop to answer in silence, but now that I've actually heard the piano plays from the girls and listened to the lyrics, it's actually a really good song, combining the actual meme of the Anime main character's social anxiety issues but also, to show that hint of romance and crush and whatever makes of love these days, it's great. So yeah, as the first chorus says: "misunderstanding (this song) is fine, is fine" after all.
Rating: 4 stars (Super cool Above Average)

Conclusion: I've not been impressed with a single for a while, and this comes in as a fresh yet familiar feel that I know I will no doubt sink into in the near future. There isn't much to explore for now, but as they continue their journey beyond their 2nd album, I know I can find something that might get into my alley, especially with this much piano and emotion you can't find anywhere else. For this, I would say ignore the Anime, for Kitri is definitely a lot more than you can imagine.

-Kaineng, on the Klassic Note
