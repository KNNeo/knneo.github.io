The Klassic Note Review 2021.09

Yeah, this review is also backdated just because haha

Well, I wish we were talking about new releases even singles, but nowadays it's just digital singles doing all the work and they don't even bother to release CDs anymore and instead come up with other types of media like vinyl and cassette(?) so yeah, I just went back to give a single from 8 years ago lol. But hey, at least I'm still giving something.

This time around though, I am NOT giving another SCANDAL single since I didn't like the song haha, I'm continuing the journey of Taketatsu Ayana's solo music career... 8 years after since she started around 9 years ago, so yeah, I missed out A LOT. Well, I didn't miss out on everything, I just didn't give this one song so this release can sit on my shelf. But now that it has... let's talk about it!




音楽 The Klassic Note Review
Taketatsu Ayana - Shuumatsu Cinderella [Single]
Limited Edition Regular Edition
(Do you even, have any idea, how adorable Ayachi looks, when she was younger?)

Origin: Hmm where shall I start.... Introducing Taketatsu Ayana, this is her 4th single from December 2013, which also means I should also mention it is close to her 10th year singing songs as a solo artist, and so far, it has been great. With her releasing a concept best album featuring songs titled after food, giving one of her older songs brought me back to this single which it was part of. In a nutshell, this single is like many of her releases, bringing much happiness and emotion all coupled with her adorable singing voice, and now I ponder how long I've taken to get back to this and experience all of what she had to offer.

(1) Shuumatsu Cinderella [Weekend Cinderella]
From first verse:
Didn't have time to (party) in the weekend
(Even) on rest days there isn't time to sleep
Really, just today is fine, right?
This is a happy song. This is likely a party song, going from the lyrics, but if you have never heard of a genre called ska, all the years listening to ska music from Japan probably prepared me for this masterpiece even back in 2013. Because with the quirks in her singing and an adorably lovable voice, this is only a song to love. The music video only made a better impression out of this song, putting her in the fitting role of a princess after a day's work? It feels like a reality check even within the lyrics, taking time off a hectic lifestyle to party, play along, and have a great night. This song is all about having a good time, if you're in for her cuteness. The overall package of this song definitely did capture that mood, and I'm glad to have heard of it.
Rating: 4 stars (Lovingly Average)

(2) Marshmallow
From last chorus:
Someday (I) want to talk to you, about my feelings
If it delivers, (I) want to know more about you
Wait a little while more, but slowly
It's very like me, (to) softly, softly fall in love (with you)
And yes, with any release, the B-side is always the deal breaker, especially if it took me this long to even give it. At this point, I wonder why I've hesitated giving this song anyways, and I have concluded that it is likely due to me missing her singing, just in general. The lyrics made little sense talking about downloading apps with only a slight mention of mom-made marshmallows, so it really makes you wonder how much this would have been in the hearts of people who understood or even listened to it, even if it made sense it was about the dessert. For me though, I guess I really miss falling in love with her voice, and this song had a lot of that, so it definitely satisfied something in me that made me sit down to give this song because it isn't even bad. I would like to blame it on her subpar singing but since we have come this far... You're welcome, Ayachi.
Rating: 4 stars (Simply Average)

Conclusion: It's hard to even find a single release as nice as this in recent years, and I think this nostalgia trip back did teach me a thing or two. Even back then, songs are uniquely different and fresh, representing different moods and emotions, and that overall packaging and a solid melody is something many artists even lack of today. If you have been a fan of Anime songs, her solo music career will not disappoint, and her first three and now fourth release(s) are an easy recommendation to get into her as I have back then. Feel for this journey, and the rest will make you like what is Taketatsu Ayana for the past decade.

-Kaineng, on the Klassic Note
