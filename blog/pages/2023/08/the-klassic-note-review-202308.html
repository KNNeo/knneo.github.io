Anyhow, this is probably the most predictable release I've given thus far even in the history of Klassic Note I would say, as we are technically talking about an Anime's theme songs all sung by an artist that is not Hoshimachi Suisei as I've been relating to so far. *laughs* Fret not, she is nothing like her this one here is actually 16 lmao *dabs*, so let's see what is about her that made this something to talk about!




THE KLASSIC NOTE REVIEW
Tsuzuri - NAME [Single]
Tsuzuri Limited Edition Anime Regular Edition
(I mean, what do you want me to say about this? I guess the artist image looks great for another single we don't get to see the face of)

Origin: I don't think I know a lot of this 16-year-old virtual singer singing for an Anime major label, but I do know about the Anime, so maybe we talk about that. As the new season of Anime appeared and I have to deal with FIVE sequels I shall not mention of that anymore, "SukiMega" was the only series I picked up that was new, and so did I get fascinated by the arrangement of the opening and ending theme songs, especially the opening which is why we are here. Ironically, they intend to release both singles picking up each other's songs, and make this a big selling point backed up by composers with fame. And that's how I get to know this voice from the opening, and that it reminded me of VTuber Hoshimachi Suisei and her singing, still does sound uniquely different especially in the Anime music industry to stand out among the rest. Let's find out more why this is actually so.

(1) NAME
From last chorus:
"(I) want to get closer, closer
(I) will help with anything, because I'll protect you
Always always be by (your) side even if (we) get lost, (I'm) sure to find each other

(Our) hearts are fluttered and moved, does this feel weird
(I) want to know the name of this feeling"
This song just spells love in itself of course credited by the mood of the Anime itself of protecting someone without their glasses but this goes in a more generic direction, talking about how that brings them together and stuff. Melody-wise I think it's catchy enough to catch anyone's attention, but I'm particularly disturbed by the abrupt pauses especially till the end of the song where if you did not count your beats, you would be blasted without realizing which can prove to be irritating at times, but otherwise even the bridge part is smartly written I assume thanks to DECO*27 who wrote the lyrics. All around great feels though, definitely something that will get its name at some point.
Rating: Strikingly Above Average

(2) Metamoru [Metamour]
From first chorus:
"If (I) call this "affection"
Then "love" (should be) more sophisticated
Ah I don't know! Because (I'm) not yet there
(I'm) in the process between adulthood and (being a) kid"
I was initially weirded out by the song title, but now that I've listened to the song and its lyrics, I think this made sense. This is also a love song, no surprises there, but what struck me and probably youngsters out there is probably the heavy pop style which ironically did not deter me mainly because I've been listening to such genres over the years and am actually used to the technopop style it is repeating chorus after chorus, I am actually bopping to it at this point. The lyrics talk about stuck between being a kid and adult, so of course the feelings of love will be choppy and even misleading at times, and that love might be more sophisticated, and so the feelings then will be all over. Interesting take on the lyrics, a heavy pop feel that's hard to beat, short and concise makes this a better song to recommend than even the title song.
Rating: Addictingly Above Average

(3) Megane-Go-Round [Glasses-Go-Round]
This ending theme song definitely sounds like a pun waiting to happen, but that's what happens when you do an unusual collaboration between the main couple's voices and Anime singer-songwriter Ooishi Masayoshi because the latter wrote the song. In general though, this song is as normal as it's decently good and even catchy in melody and the bridge part is fine, the lyrics makes sense tying in to the theme of the Anime of seeing way more without glasses in (because of people around you), just that this version is technically a cover song done by the opening theme artist herself as the others have done for her song too. So yeah, no special dialogues, no extra harmony, choruses are essentially just her, all that flair. Her singing does bring in the usual mature feminine voice not in the original though, which probably makes this cover all that unique.
Rating (original song): Lovingly Above Average

Conclusion: This will be a rare recommendation even from me to a virtual singer at this time of age, but heck, I'll do it. This is a good release overall, and even if you have no idea of the Anime series, this will still make of a great start to listen to the new Anime singer in town who is trying to make a name (get it?) for herself at a young age. Hopefully with such a head start she can go far into the Anime music industry, because honestly saying? This start isn't too bad at all.


- Aozaki Shouhei, on the Klassic Note
