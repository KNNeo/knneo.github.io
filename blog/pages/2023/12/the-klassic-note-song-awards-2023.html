The Klassic Note Song Awards 2023
üéäWELCOME TO THE KLASSIC NOTE SONG AWARDS 2023!!üéä
I'm Aozaki Shouhei, Klassic Note's only listener and judge. It's that time of the year again, where I sit down in front of my PC at the end of the year to commend and rank the songs I listen over the year. This year is a year of change, but unlike what was with 15th year anniversary last year, I have marked this year as a year of more drastic change. Following artists I've liked over the year but have not performed well will be shelved over time, as many have announced their hiatus or even brought their music careers into an abrupt end. On the other hand, I am more focused on giving new, prominent artists which I would have not followed just because of a hit or two. Nonetheless, we will find more artists to listen to, as I continue to branch out even further towards styles of my interest, being more strict in reviewing releases and avoiding subpar songs here and then. This will hopefully help to bring forward a paradigm shift towards melodies I would prefer more regardless of presentation, its lyrics, or even its style, following our new motto of "Searching For The New Classic".

As usual, I will state this every year and I will state it again, the results below do not fully represent the many songs I listen to for the year. Past year songs are typically suppressed since recommending them is not as trending as the ones who are presented for this year, so I will withhold in promoting them, though when they are, it's always because they have made more than impact in my life. New artists are still sprouting as they seem, sadly being critical of them unlike how the earlier artists I listen to, would not help in their cause, but as to stay with my motto and criticism, I will continue to find ones that I like, if they still stand to impress. Overall, this is just a fraction of what is to come, and what I think of them songs and artists for the year.

This year, I have given a total of 246 songs over the past 12 months. I've spent more time on each release longer compared to past years in recognition of their efforts even listening to them the second time in a year, and find comfort in giving familiar artists and new artists I picked up alike. The Internet might have helped with exposure even more, and with music streaming services it has become easier than ever to give any recommendation I see them on YouTube and/or Apple Music, hopefully providing than enough bandwidth to listen to classics of newly found artists and their new songs too. It definitely did not stop the momentum of giving new songs though; I am just giving more songs per release, more often than ever.

READ MORE
Firstly, I break down the songs to who sings them and sort them by artist composition and gender, giving a good look into a usual heavy bias of female solo artists singing good songs, also known as the Vocals Popularity Survey. For this year, Female Solo vocals have risen to the yearly average of 1.3 to 1 against Male Solo vocals, which shows a nice balance of mainstream artists against female solo artists for the year.

Then, we have the Song Rating Integrity Survey. Initially taken as an experimental analysis in Klassic Note to fix my biased opinions of what is a good song, now it is used as a guide to follow past years judgement criteria so my judgement don't go too far off the perpendicular. As I give further detailed analysis to the many songs defaulted to the generic 4-star category, this survey then is an attempt to give a better insight into how most of the songs are rated. It is also became the new benchmark in recent years in pushing me to give more 5-star songs which were then based on early impression which is not possible now with maturity of my song judgement bias. As you can see, there is higher tendency to give more 4-star songs as songs are rated slightly above average instead of promoting them to 5-stars.

Thirdly, we have the generic statistics of the songs of the year. These include language (for those with a vocalist), year of release (old songs can appear in the list as far as 2005 for the year), Anime songs (because when I say "sometimes" on Twitter I meant this) and B-side analysis for singles which is a direct representation of proving that songs which are good need not be their release's topic songs, and we should give more criticism and praise to songs not under intentional spotlight.

Last but not least, we have the Song Appetite Report. The chart represents how many songs I've given every month, and show peak and trough periods typically based on release month dependent on the music industry or periods of time where I just want to give more or less songs. I would say despite the difficulties and therefore delays in releasing new songs this particular year, many still were released in due time, and therefore did not miss much in terms of leaving an impression in Klassic Note, and I am thankful for that. Exceptions do apply though, where certain releases are still out of reach with the return of love event-only releases.

Klassic Note may be all about the songs, but what is with songs if they aren't released in some form? I say that though, but with the rise of digital singles and how music is distributed more often through digital platforms in smaller doses instead of coming up with physical releases, it's always in my comfort to listen to albums every once in a while then be committed to their constant promotions. Many releases performed better than ever as I spend more time with them this year, and I continue to give more songs beyond the one or two topic songs with a music video. Many artists are going for more than one good album in the year with the world opening up, and we do see this year some are adding a couple of singles and/or new songs from time to time within the year. Regardless of their approach, I will still give them due attention as long as I know of them. Limited Edition Only singles are great but sadly does not meet my requirement of Review, but I will still talk put them in just because they are worthy of mention.

In Klassic Note, top singles are determined by (1) whether they are in Klassic Note Review ie. all songs of the single have been given and is not digital only release and (2) how good they fare in impression, so ratings come to place. Details for giving these singles shall not be discussed further since there is The Klassic Note Review for your reference. Top albums are determined mostly by how many songs have been given in it, so best album have a natural advantage if I've been listening to the artist for years, and mini-albums tend to get an edge on these due to low song count too but they have been put lower in priority to allow full length albums to shine.

From Klassic Note 2023, I will also annotate special features of these releases (Limited Edition Only, Digital Only) in order to better represent their prominence in the ranks, and that smaller releases in albums, for example, will get smaller priority over full album which require more effort (subject to the amount of new songs added) so the latter will be considered first.

One of the most important things that built the foundation of this Song Awards are of course, the Awards themselves. They have also been historically the toughest to decide every year apart from the Top 20 itself, which shows how much thought is put into figuring out the distinct feels of all the songs I give for the year, and how important they are compared to all of the others above. As with previous years, it has always been a challenge to get the right songs to fit the Awards and what they should represent, and as much as I try I still hardly feel for some of these songs which are worthy of their titles, especially pertaining to certain emotions and moods. Regardless, I have made my decision, so here are the results.

Congratulations to Homecomings for THE CONTRIBUTOR AND THE ARTIST awards because damn, have I been immersed too much in their songs for the year, especially from their indie days. THE MUSIC award also met its strongest competition for the year, as immersion into Jazz Funk group Sai have made me intoxicated on occult styles I normally would not give despite PHONO TONES and SPECIAL OTHERS releasing albums to please for the year.

The Award titles mean exactly unless stated otherwise, which are as follows:
  • THE CHARASON represents all songs from the Anime and Game genres so to give more songs recognition on how good and/or diverse they are (CV refers to "Character Voice" ie. the actual voice actor behind the role)
  • THE IDOLISM awards idol songs (sung by voice actress who became solo artists mostly) which have been on the rise and the award goes to the one with judgement possible to be independent of usual criteria eg. melody, music style
  • THE INSPIRATIONAL awards songs which spark motivation and/or creativity for me, encouraging and pushing me along in life
  • THE NUMBER ONE SONG OF THE YEAR is based on SONG OF THE DAY results, and the Award is presented to the best here
  • THE SINGLE and THE ALBUM are based on RELEASE RANKINGS, and the award is presented to the best of each category here
The nominees are excluded from the list due to presentation space constraints.

Here are my thoughts about this year's Top 20 as elaborated from above, in a nutshell:
  • Homecomings position in the Top 20 list has already been cemented since February with me deep into their indie releases, and the new album definitely helped in topping up that fuel I would have never expected to deal with. Still, they deserve every bit of it, they have the unique emotional feels that no other band can achieve, it has been a few years since their last and I have been wanting to give them something, and I'm glad I can get into this over time.
  • The next few ranks are my tribute to the artists who made an impression this year alone, including now regular mol-74, Kroi, and even Kodama Hikari is in the ranks.
  • I would have thought of putting Ikimonogakari in a better rank preferably above my own bias Natsukawa Shiina who killed it with the new album this year, I still think the latter deserved to be up there purely because they gave us a rather simple melody to satisfy, I will say that much.
  • Ryokuoushoku Shakai dropped real bad this year considering their overall assessment even including their rather unimpressive though unique new album this year, I was even struggling on considering what to give them this year because seriously, even the summer song did not manage to impress me for that long. The same is to say for regular artists in my Collection including SHE'S and LUCK LIFE who have been downplayed, and cinema staff who have been left on the lurch this year just because.
  • This year's double ranks are for fanservice I did not want to leave Fuchigami Mai alone so I slotted in Kitou Akari (or maybe it's vice versa), and I don't know if putting miwa long with emotion giant and veteran aiko-san would mean anything but hey, both respectable artists I would put my finger on, so you are welcome, ladies.
  • Ironically because of the awards above, one would think otherwise I would put Uesaka Sumire with Kitou Akari but my biggest concern about giving her more recognition after I presented her the best single award for the year, is that is a very average release and because of the single containing more tracks (and I gave them all), that her subpar singing would otherwise take shape in the ranks. This is as far as I will praise herm honestly.
