アニメ Kaguya-sama wa Kokurasetai?
"You just want us to win such that we don't make Iino Miko a laughing stock?" Haha Shirogane, didn't know you can read human minds, out of all of them.
Man, with all this seriousness, I can't get over the fact that we aren't even getting a joke dropped down half this episode, but yeah, this is what happens when you get a second season Anime adaptation haha. So, it's time to get serious. In this new episode (#KaguyaSamaS206) we reach the final part of the Elections arc I would like to say, and it ended spectacularly, in many ways then other. Firstly, Shirogane's edge. Obviously, through many means than other, he is at the winning side, mostly due to Kaguya being scheming as heck even to the extent of bribing and getting teaches to stare at students who are not on their side (wow, just wow), but that's not the point. On Miko-chan's turn, that's where the focus is that this time around. We have learnt that Miko-chan has never won Student Council elections thus far, not even once. For being too serious, she is never liked by anyone other than her best friend since she started school. That said, when standing upon the stage for her own speech, that's where everything including her past and present hit her. Not the awesome grades, not her confidence, but the lack thereof. One should know even without listening to her backstory as explained by her friend that, over the course of so many years ever she started running for Student Council elections, she has been infamously... bullied, in many fronts. I'm not going to start sh*tting on her because I am one myself to a smaller extent, but I would argue life treats all of us differently, and evil is mostly what was placed upon Miko-chan. Looking at you, Kaguya-sama. *laughs* But going back to Ishigami's request at the start of the episode, he stated one thing that he wanted to do, and that is to win the other candidate by a landslide. Though rather vague, he didn't explain why until he showed Shirogane her stage fright due to all the years of trying to break through, and if you didn't have that of a conclusion from Shirogane... I hope you have learnt a lesson too. Because really, Miko-chan has suffered enough. Just like Kaguya but she never changes so I won't care now refer to season 1 for that haha, but suffice it to say that helping Miko-chan is better than killing her. Mentally, I mean. It takes a lot to voice out what you want (ignore those protesters please, I swear), but as an individual, doing that requires a lot more than confidence. Finding that new comfort zone is never as easy as doing it forever until you get it. Coming out from all these years of growing, I can say that too. So Shirogane dropped the bomb on Ishigami seeing how he was pissed at her, is that they should help her learn. And thus, Shirogane, instead of cutting her off just as she was speechless at her speech after Kaguya literally railed on her election speech, chose to let her present her point to the students in the school so she could get her point across. Though she did not win, the students then learnt of how bad the prestigious school's reputation was on morals ie. rock bottom, and why there is a need to improve on it, basically her as a member of the Morals Committee. But yeah, seriously, Shirogane's team has been enjoying life too much in school. Didn't they say the worst of life is at school? These kids look like they're on a vacation lmao.
