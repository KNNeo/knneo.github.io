Overall, it was a good lesson to learn. As you can see, production wise it's spectacular, I thought audio was an issue until I realised what happened lol (prolonged use of the cat mask makes you one step closer to being one) (or maybe I'm turning into a cat lmao), the premise of the story in general is pretty good. It was rather complete, it covered a lot of aspects of Miyo's life and even Hinode's to the finest of detail which I'm impressed, talking about teenage dreams and love and everything in between, even friendship as seen by Miyo's relationship with her bestie. I'm thrown away by Miyo's bubbly personality (even though we know of her true self), the Mask Seller's scheming looks even as a cat haha that was actually impressive, how I can recognise the voice of Urahara Kisuke in this movie (yes it's the wisest in the bunch of cats who saved all of them), how I didn't expect Yoriko the friend to be involved in the story so much (I guess it's because of talking about loved ones all of them are heavily involved) (hey I get more Mina-chan so that's good hehe), and lastly, how much the story ties in together. Not that I don't see logic flaws, but I would argue they did explain the story well enough for you to not doubt about everything else. Finally, this thing is a tearjerker. So prepare some tissues; you're gonna need it. And don't "Aoharu ka yo" me because this one isn't a joke. No really, it's a good one. I really enjoyed it. And now even as the theme songs are imprinting on me playing in loop until I finish writing this (lol), I didn't regret picking this one up. My initial impression was really, only from the one trailer which was a "meh" to me, but I think I miss these movies. It's not Studio Ghibli (they don't do these anymore) (Spirited Away, I mean), it doesn't have to go too far, but this is the kind of Anime I want to watch, to be honest.
