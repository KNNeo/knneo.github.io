SID - delete [Single]
Limited EditionRegular EditionAnime Limited Edition
(I mean, you tell me which cover is cooler haha definitely not the Anime)

Origin: We should not be unfamiliar with this J-Rock band already. In Klassic Note, that means one of the earliest artist around since they started their major debut under music label Sony Music Japan (specifically Ki/oon Records), and since back then they have been on a roll, from Anime theme songs to songs of their own... they have excelled to gain national attention. But as the years go by and as new waves of artists, I feel that they have lost their touch, both in singing new songs and gaining fame as much as they did a decade ago. Until recently maybe last year did they pick up their pace with further social media appeal, did they then make an effort to come back to live. With this newest Anime tie-up they have done in a while and also being a regular artist of the series, they have set themselves to break barriers and show how cool they have been, and they are still today.

(1) delete
"In the nights that (I) can't sleep, how many dreams have (I) seen?
Until daybreak, (I'm) barely surviving"
This is literally SID strikes back as it hits all the right beats and is such a great introduction to the style of this band, I can't even describe it better myself. From the rock beats, to the meaningful lyrics especially when you have to kind of be different for an action series, to that guitarist solo... everything is awesome about them. Their image as a boys' band may not hit as much today, but I can assure you that the music in them is as unique for them as it is great for all of us. I couldn't recommend more. It's not their best, but it's one of those solid hits that you don't want to miss.
Rating: 4 stars (Cooler Than Anime Average)

Conclusion: This is a simple release in itself, and I think any more and I may not feel comfortable. But this is the kind of stuff that make me want to buy CDs in the first place: they leave a good impression, they are worth the many listens in the near future and beyond... a true classic. It's not much, but it makes you remember. And for SID, this is many of their wonderful compositions I've come to appreciate, and I know you will too.


- Kaineng, on the Klassic Note
