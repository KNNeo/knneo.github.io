The Klassic Note Song Awards - Official Results (2018)

Welcome to the Klassic Note Song Awards 2018!

I'm Kaineng, Klassic Note's only listener and judge. Every year, giving out awards and displaying those decisions has always been a challenge. This year is no exception as I move on to the next stage of my life in adulthood. As I continue to listen to more new artists while reminiscing the passing of many artists within the year, it motivates me more to further search for better songs to make this a better experience and involvement in my life.

Once again, the results below do not fully represent the many songs I listen to for the year. More indie artists have surfaced thanks to YouTube videos, mainstream artists are saying their farewells including uprising ones too, and I still cannot stop myself from giving Anime and idol-based songs as they continue to creep into the ranks this year. A big thanks again to another year of wonderful songs from all artists who got in the list, and there wouldn't be Klassic Note without any of them, but of course, give yourself a pat on the shoulder if your song(s) ever appeared in this results page somehow. I tried my best to give recognition to as many artists as I can every year.

The report below will be split into six segments, with the first two covering statistics and analysis of the songs given this year and how they fare. Diagrams will be shown in some segments to give a better overall picture than just displaying tables of data. Finally, I'll conclude on the report with an audio edit of all Top 20 ranked songs of this year, so to give a better idea of what songs truly represent the year around.

(Results finalised on 31/12/2018)

Segment 1 – Song List Statistics, Song Integrity Survey & Song Appetite Report

This year, I have given a total of 205 songs over the past 12 months. Not as much as last year, but still very significant considering the number of new artists that have graced their presence in Klassic Note this year.

Firstly, I break down the songs to who sings them and sort them by size and gender, giving a good looks to a certain bias of hearing just female artists sing good songs, also known as the Vocal Styles Popularity Survey. For 2018, Female Solo vocals are close to a two-to-one ratio against Male Solo vocals, which shows a consistent improvement of reducing vocalist bias over the years.

Then, we have the Song Integrity Survey. Taken as an experimental analysis in Klassic Note, here I give further detailed analysis to the many songs rated in the generic 4-star category to give a better insight into how most of the songs are rated. It is also became the new benchmark in recent years in pushing to give more 5-star songs based on impression as done in early years of Klassic Note. As you can see, there is bias to giving more 5-star songs as songs above average rank are more than below average. Also, starting from next year, the categories for 4-stars will be increased from three to four, with 4C being average in order to show lesser bias to the standard average rating.

Thirdly, we have the generic statistics of the songs for the year. These include language (for those with a vocalist), year of release (old songs can appear in the list as far as 2001 for this year), Anime songs (because "sometimes") and B-side analysis for singles which is a direct representation of showing that songs which are good need not be their release's topic songs.

Lastly, we have the Song Appetite Report. The chart represents how many songs I've given every month, and whether there are peak period of giving songs or not, typically based on release month dependent on the music industry or periods of time where I just want to give more or less songs.

Segment 3 – Single & Album Rankings

What are songs without releases, right? Here we talk about the overall packaging of songs in compiled lists, also known better by their smaller and larger counterparts. Many releases did not perform well this year, which explains the number of reviewed releases this year, however I did give more songs out of the decresed reviews, which is a good thing. Best album dominate the ranks this year as many are leaving, and some want to make an impression here before they leave so I can't deny giving them chances here and there.

In Klassic Note, best singles are determined by (1) whether they are on the shelf ie. all songs of the single have been given and (2) how good they fare in impression. Details for giving these singles shall not be discussed here since there is The Klassic Note Review for your reference. Best albums are determined mostly by how many songs have been given in it, so best album have a natural advantage if I've been listening to the artist for years.

Congratulations to Sambomaster and miwa for being best single and album of 2018!

Segment 4 – Song Commendations

When I think of those worth mentioning but not necessarily worth of competition because it feels more like an achievement or special encounter, I take note. Segment 5 – Song Awards
One of the most important things that built the foundation of the song awards are of course, the awards themselves. They have also been the toughest to decide every year besides the Top 20 songs, which shows how much thought needed to be put in in order to award songs into categories every year to show what kind of features they possess, and how unique they are. Like last year, it feels really hard to even get enough songs for entry to even award one of them as I hardly feel for some of the songs which are worthy of the titles. However, I have made my case, and below are the results.

Congratulations to Mineuchi Tomomi for being THE CHARASON of the year with a character songs from Anime "Slow Start", and to GLIM SPANKY for being THE CONTRIBUTOR and THE ARTST of 2018!

The Award titles mean exactly unless stated otherwise, which are stated as follows:
  • THE CHARASON now represents all songs from the Anime and Game genres so to give more songs recognition on how good they are.
  • THE IDOLISM awards idol songs (sung by voice actress solo artists mostly) which have been on the rise and gives the one with best impressions
  • THE INSPIRATIONAL awards songs which create motivation and creativity for me, and encourage me to further heights
Some Awards may feel familiar (NUMBER ONE SONG [OF THE DAY], ALBUM, SINGLE) because they are taken from previous segments, and awards are given to the best in their category
