No seriously what's with the dope opening theme song lol okay this show. And hoho I like this show has the gossip 'cos what are you going to do in the Edo period (covering 16th-17th century) anyways? Firstly, some context as to why I'm watching this. I was here because of Mikakoshi. *laughs* Okay, I was here because she tweeted about her in this show. So yeah, and it's been some time since I watched a historical-based mystery Anime, so here I am. And boy, am I wanting to continue with this series, it's quite interesting. Now, the first episode (#Tsukumogami01) makes me think that the tsukumogami are like a paparazzi squad where they can go eavesdrop on others to help solve mysteries in the district. Then aside, the story goes of a rental shop (in this case it's not just specific items like DVDs(?), bicycles, umbrellas (lol China) and cars etc.) who lends items to people who might have a need for it. Say, you want a change in accessories. You don't have to keep buying them because in this pawn shop you can "sell" your current one and switch it with another, technically speaking. So yeah. But the exception in this Edo period shop is, many of them are possessed by gods. (We're not talking about single-entity overlords here like Jesus and Buddha, mind you) Gods which manifest out of care of the items treasured by humans. Amongst them are (I'm going Yachiru naming mode here because I don't remember their names) bat brat, moon head, fake smoker, miniature female shop owner and hair comb rabbit lol. They live in the shop Seiji and Okou are in (the latter being the female elder sister here), and first up we have... well this is literally a gossip episode so I'll leave it for the tsukimogami to give you some sweet headlines, but yeah, it's a marriage come unwanted. So... you know we're going to hear this often, but yeah, if you had no choice but to sell your treasured items for survival, you gotta do it. That's too bad. On the other hand, just die with it already the god in it is more important than you haha. Lastly, I would like to compliment on how well this show was done. The animation is great, the story is told well with modern society references in case you never lived in the Edo period in Japan lol (they need to explain the koku thing though), and there's sick theme songs to get the kids in. All in all, it may make of quite a history lesson that I've never seen before, and this might take be on a journey. My last time was that Koisento OVA back in 2010 which technically is not an accurate depiction of history anyways. but yeah, I expected that kind of theme songs. So yeah, sorry Shishido Kavka; Kuraki Mai will be next on my music list.
If you like just any, any one these cute characters feel free to watch this you won't be disappointed
